<h2><a href="http://androidoperatingsystem.net/2010/03/17/att-%e2%80%93-not-really-getting-in-to-the-spirit-of-android/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to AT&amp;T – not really getting in to the spirit of Android">AT&amp;T – not really getting in to the spirit of Android</a></h2>
<div class="date"> <span class="author">Posted by codetastrophe</span> <span class="clock">   March - 17 - 2010 - Wednesday 
					<div class="category"><a href="http://androidoperatingsystem.net/category/andriod/" title="View all posts in Andriod" rel="category tag">Andriod</a> </div>


</span> <span class="comm"><a href="http://androidoperatingsystem.net/2010/03/17/att-%e2%80%93-not-really-getting-in-to-the-spirit-of-android/#respond" title="Comment on AT&amp;T – not really getting in to the spirit of Android">ADD COMMENTS</a></span> </div>	
</div>

<div class="cover">
<div class="entry">
					<div class="translate_block" style="display: none;">
							<a class="translate_translate" id="translate_button_post-52391" lang="en" xml:lang="en" href="javascript:show_translate_popup('en', 'post', 52391);">[Translate this post into any language - Click Here]<img src="http://androidoperatingsystem.net/wp-content/plugins/google-ajax-translation/transparent.gif" id="translate_loading_post-52391" class="translate_loading" style="display: none;" width="16" height="16" alt=""></a>
							<hr class="translate_hr">
						</div>
						<p>
</p><blockquote>
<p><em><strong>“Innovating in the open</strong></em><em><br>
Each member of the Open Handset Alliance is strongly committed to greater openness in the mo</em><em>bile ecosystem. Increased openness will enable everyone in our industry to innovate more rapidly and respond better to consumers’ demands. Our first joint project as a new Alliance is Android™. Android was built from the ground up with the explicit goal to be the first open, complete, and free platform created specifically for mobile devices.”</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Some of you out there will recognise the text above; it’s from the <a href="http://www.androidoperatingsystem.net/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/a95ac69a7eLocked2.jpg2.jpg"><img class="size-full wp-image-1432 alignright" title="Backflip_Locked" src="http://www.androidoperatingsystem.net/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/a95ac69a7eLocked2.jpg2.jpg" alt="" width="200" height="214"></a>Open Handset Alliance’s overview text.&nbsp; That, right there, details one of the main strengths of Android: openness.&nbsp; There are some though, who, whilst happy to jump on the Android bandwagon (hey, it’s hot right now, we’ll sell some phones off the back of the hype, right?) would far rather that you didn’t have any of this, “open and free platform” nonsense.&nbsp; Take AT&amp;T for example: on their first Android device, the Motorola BACKFLIP, not only have they locked out Google as the default search option, they’ve also disabled installation of all apps from sources other than the official Android Market.&nbsp; Now, at first glance, that may not seem like such a big deal.&nbsp; Annoying and unsurprising from AT&amp;T, yes, but not the end of the world, right?</p>
<p>When you dig a bit deeper however, it opens up a whole bucketfull of worms.&nbsp; If users can’t install non-Android Market apps, how does a developer get BACKFLIP owners to alpha or beta test his or her apps?&nbsp; How often have we seen apps that have device-specific issues?&nbsp; No developer will know for certain if their app works on the BACKFLIP if no-one, themselves included, can install the app on the phone until it’s published.</p>
<p>Worse still, if you are an enterprise customer who planned on issuing the BACKFLIP to some of your staff, you can’t just roll out published apps from your corporate servers.&nbsp; Whilst there are probably not that many companies using Android apps developed in-house as yet, I expect that, with how open Android is <em>meant </em>to be, there soon will be.&nbsp; It will impact on the developers of enterprise products too.&nbsp; Take <a href="http://www.nitrodesk.com/index.aspx" target="_blank">NitroDesk</a>, developers of the Exchange sync app, Touchdown.&nbsp; They recently made a <a href="http://nitrodesk.blogspot.com/" target="_blank">blogpost</a> detailing their growing concerns over the situation-</p>
<blockquote><p><em>(one of the main attractions of Android is…) “the ability for an enterprise to publish an application to their intranet/extranet site and have users point their device browser to it to install it on their devices. This is pretty powerful in the future if you expect enterprises to adopt android en masse, publishing their internal applications without having the whole world see it and rate it and post spam on its comments.”</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p>They go on to add-</p>
<blockquote><p><em>“I really hope this practice stops with this particular device, and does not spread to other phones and other carriers.. it will be the beginning of the end for Android.”</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p>The development community aren’t the only ones upset about the walled garden that is the BACKFLIP.&nbsp; As well as a steadily growing <a href="http://www.facebook.com/group.php?gid=363063913320&amp;ref=mf" target="_blank">Remove the Motorola Backflip’s Restrictions</a> community which has popped up on Facebook, there are also a number of forums, such as the <a href="http://forums.wireless.att.com/cng/board/message?board.id=motorola&amp;thread.id=110497&amp;view=by_date_ascending&amp;page=2" target="_blank">AT&amp;T</a> and <a href="https://supportforums.motorola.com/thread/24951?tstart=60" target="_blank">Motorola </a>forums, which are seeing BACKFLIP owners voicing their opinions on the subject.&nbsp; One user, veasley, on the AT&amp;T forums commented-</p>
<blockquote><p><em> “It is a big deal, to me, that google was removed because I use google on my windows phone a lot and on my desktop almost exclusively. I have been waiting for both android and palm to show up on ATT but it is discouraging to see what they did to the Backflip. Verizon, who had a reputation for doing this sort of thing, has allowed Android phones to be Androids. That is pretty enticing. I have been with ATT for almost a decade and never considered leaving before but after growing weary of windows phones and watching all the really fun smart phones going to other carriers (though the iphone is a great phone in my book) I was elated to see ATT branch out a little. When I discovered before the release date the default browser for the Backflip would be Yahoo I decided to wait for Palm to arrive. If the Palm phone(s) receive the same treatment I will go to Verizon. I don’t begrudge ATT for making what they consider good business decisions. They have the right as a private enterprise to do so. What I am saying is that if they continue denying the options I want in a phone ATT becomes a bad fit for me. I have a feeling I am not the only one who feel this way.”</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p>It’ll be interesting to see if AT&amp;T continue this trend with future Android phones.&nbsp; I suspect they will, but of more concern is whether other carriers, having seen AT&amp;T try it, will consider similar measures in future.&nbsp; I sincerely hope not.&nbsp; Of course some carriers (for example, Sprint and T-Mobile) are members of the Open Handset Alliance, and so they would be unable (and, one assumes, unwilling) to lock things down in such a fashion, but if big players in the US like AT&amp;T and Verizon steer their Android offerings in this stifling direction, it could have disastrous effects for Android in the long term.</p>

<p><a href="http://www.androidoperatingsystem.net/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/1304eea98d1-SDK1.jpg.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-183" title="Android-21-SDK" src="http://www.androidoperatingsystem.net/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/4fdc6655be00x294.jpg.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="294"></a></p>
<p>You’ll no doubt have heard the news that Google are giving out free Nexus One or Motorola DROID phones to Android developers who have apps with over 5,000 downloads and an average rating over 3.5 stars.&nbsp; There were initially some concerns that this was a hoax designed to get developers’ personal details which prompted comments and tweets from Google officials which went some way to ease the worries, but there were still a few developers out there who were wary.&nbsp; Well, Mario from <a href="http://www.badlogicgames.com">Badlogic Games</a> got in touch yesterday to say that he has received his confirmation email, direct from Google.&nbsp; So it seems any reamining doubters can rest easy.&nbsp; Here’s what you lucky developers can expect to receive-</p>
<blockquote><p><em>Dear Mario,</em></p>
<p><em>We’ve received your information for the Android Market Device Seeding Program and have successfully validated the Google Order Number from your developer account.</em></p>
<p><em>Your information will now be sent to our shipping partner for order processing. Just to confirm, the information we received from you was:</em></p>
<p><em>Badlogic Games</em></p>
<p><em>XXX my personal data XXX</em></p>
<p><em>If you need to make any changes to your information above, please contact us at <a href="mailto:android-market-seeding@google.com" target="_blank">android-market-seeding@google.com</a> as soon as possible. Otherwise, you should receive your phone in 2-4 weeks!</em></p>
<p><em>On behalf of the Android team,<br>
Thanks, and happy coding!</em></p>
</blockquote>

<h2><a href="http://androidoperatingsystem.net/2010/03/02/scam-alert-haleron-ilet-mini-hal-%e2%80%93-too-good-to-be-true-after-all/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Scam alert -Haleron iLet Mini HAL – too good to be true after all?">Scam alert -Haleron iLet Mini HAL – too good to be true after all?</a></h2>
<div class="date"> <span class="author">Posted by webnull</span> <span class="clock">   March - 2 - 2010 - Tuesday 
					<div class="category"><a href="http://androidoperatingsystem.net/category/andriod/" title="View all posts in Andriod" rel="category tag">Andriod</a> </div>


</span> <span class="comm"><a href="http://androidoperatingsystem.net/2010/03/02/scam-alert-haleron-ilet-mini-hal-%e2%80%93-too-good-to-be-true-after-all/#comments" title="Comment on Scam alert -Haleron iLet Mini HAL – too good to be true after all?">9 COMMENTS</a></span> </div>	
</div>

<div class="cover">
<div class="entry">
					<div class="translate_block" style="display: none;">
							<a class="translate_translate" id="translate_button_post-48671" lang="en" xml:lang="en" href="javascript:show_translate_popup('en', 'post', 48671);">[Translate this post into any language - Click Here]<img src="http://androidoperatingsystem.net/wp-content/plugins/google-ajax-translation/transparent.gif" id="translate_loading_post-48671" class="translate_loading" style="display: none;" width="16" height="16" alt=""></a>
							<hr class="translate_hr">
						</div>
						<p></p>
<p><a href="http://www.androidoperatingsystem.net/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/c964acd866ni_hal.jpg.jpg"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1123" title="haleron_ilet-Mini_hal" src="http://www.androidoperatingsystem.net/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/c964acd866ni_hal.jpg.jpg" alt="" width="495" height="351"></a></p>
<p>A few days ago, we reported that a company called Haleron were going to be releasing an indifferently spec’ed, but temptingly cheap, 7″ Android tablet.  Well, the folks over at <a href="http://www.pocketables.net/2010/02/haleron-ilet-mini-hal-the-latest-device-to-join-the-7inch-tablet-army.html#comment-6a00d83451c9ec69e201310f40a3ce970c" target="_blank">Pocketables</a> have cast some doubt on the authenticity of the offer.&nbsp; A commenter called Brad had this to say:-</p>
<div>
<blockquote><p>In an attempt to verify Haleron a month or two ago, I tracked the iLet design back to a french company which has an active forum. They in fact do sell the design to be remanufactured abroad. I inquired if they had licensed to Haleron, and received a solid NO.</p>
<p>If you look at the whois on the haleron domain, it is registered to a shipping district office in Miami Fl. Google the CEO’s name and you get several other failed companies, and a business bankruptcy filing in Colorado.</p>
<p>Based on my research, I believe the company is a scam.</p>
</blockquote>
<p><a href="http://gizmodo.com/5481887/haleron-ilet-mini-hal-is-a-modest-tablet-with-a-modest-price" target="_blank">Gizmodo</a> have been investigating the claims too and, like Brad, they also seem unconvinced by Haleron’s authenticity.</p>
<p>Doing a bit of digging, it is difficult to come up with any actual evidence of Haleron as a company, and although I’m seeing a lot of mentions of kit that the company have released over the past few months or are planning to release in the future, I’ve yet to find a review of any of their actual hardware, or even a post from a satisfied customer.&nbsp; However, it’s worth mentioning that this in itself isn’t conclusive proof that we are seeing is a scam.&nbsp; For now, I’d be wary of ordering until we’ve seen more.&nbsp; The company have stated that they expect the units to be available via Amazon at some point in mid-March.&nbsp; If you’re tempted by this unit, it might be advisable to hang fire until (and if) it appears on Amazon.&nbsp; In the meantime, I’m going to continue investigating and see if I can find out the truth.</p>
<p><strong>UPDATE</strong>- I’m actively trying to find out more information on Grupo Haleron and I’ll continue to update my findings as I go along.</p>
<p>The first thing that struck me, is the support <a href="http://www.haleron.com/index.php?option=com_agora&amp;Itemid=65" target="_blank">forums</a> on the Haleron website – they have only a handful of posts, and none of them seem to come from any user who actually owns a device.&nbsp; I did find a link inside one of the posts which gives information on where to get downloads for a Swordfish netbook that they sell.&nbsp; The link is apparently for the Swordfish community pages at <a href="http://www.sfmovil.com/" target="_blank">sfmovil.com</a>.&nbsp; If you check out sfmovil.com, you’ll see it’s little more than a mobile phone review blog and has been for months judging by the archives.&nbsp; In fact, some of the archive posts go back to before the site was linked on the Haleron support forums.&nbsp; Not only that, but if you search sfmovil.com for the term “swordfish” there are no articles found at all.&nbsp; Now, all of that seems more than a little fishy to my mind.</p>
<p>I’m still waiting on someone from Haleron getting back to me on my questions.&nbsp; I’ve also tried to get in touch with someone from the office building that Haleron claim to operate from in Medellin, Colombia.</p>
<p><strong>UPDATE 2</strong>- I’ve been checking around further and found a Spanish language <a href="http://www.laneros.com/archive/index.php/t-162109.html" target="_blank">site</a>, possibly from Colombia, where the authenticity of Haleron is discussed.&nbsp; Two translated quotes there from a poster called “soldfoxdx” are of particular note-</p>
<blockquote><p>Gentlemen in the direction of HALERON monteria is located in a residential area, more exactly in the Gold Coast neighborhood CRA 16 # 28-08. I have not heard of any such company who is on there and my experience with things of this town, I can say that this seems more that the very Vaporware Phantom Console.</p>
<p>I do not think that technology exists and the engineers needed in this town of farmers and paramilitaries to make such a feat. surely are importers of Chinese pods. I will take the task of going to visit them.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>And this second, also from solidfoxdx a couple of days later-</p>
<blockquote><p>good CONFIRMED. Haleron is a ghost.</p>
<p>There is no such company at the address: CRA 16 # 28-08 Monteria – Cordoba. what exists is a teaching of English for children LOL! tigo no answer to that published (I have not tried the Movistar)</p>
</blockquote>
<p>So folks, it looks very much like this is a hoax of quite impressive proportions.</p>
<p>Thanks to Luiz Rodriguez for the heads up on this story.</p>
