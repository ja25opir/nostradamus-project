<div id="blog-comments"><a href="/1/post/2010/03/finished-dulcimer-12-started-some-more-bowls.html#comments" class="blog-link">0 Comment(s)</a></div><div id="blog-separator"> &nbsp; </div><div id="blog-author"> <b></b></div><div id="blog-content"><div class="paragraph" style=" text-align: left; ">Dulcimer #12 was finished this Saturday.&nbsp; It's a really nice playing one, and it has already been promised to a nice gentleman I met at Woodcraft.&nbsp; Since I'm sort of a varnish addict, I'm not stuck with nothing to rub finish onto.&nbsp; So last night, I started on two more bowls.&nbsp; I also have to start working on another dulcimer (#13) that my coworker wants to give to his wife, although now he's not so sure because she hasn't been playing the bass guitar he got her last.&nbsp; But in any case, I've already started on it in a sense, since the sides have been sitting in the jig for about a month already, and the scroll was made many months ago.&nbsp; This week I'll cut the fretboard, and try to finish up on my two cigar box guitars.&nbsp; This one will be cherry sides, back, fretboard and scroll with Western Red Cedar top.&nbsp; He really liked the way #10 turned out, however that one was promised to my church already.&nbsp; The action is way low on #10 because I used a zero fret.&nbsp; So far, I don't like way low action because they feel kind of lame to me, and you can't really dig in with a hearty strum, but there are people who like it, especially for finger-picking.&nbsp; I suppose if I add a pickup, these lower action dulcimers would do very well as acoustic-electric instruments.&nbsp; I'll have to try that someday.&nbsp; #14 is in the jig already, avocado sides I got from my friend Steve on ED.&nbsp; I think I'll install a pickup before closing the top on that one.</div></div><div id="blog-post-separator">&nbsp;</div>
