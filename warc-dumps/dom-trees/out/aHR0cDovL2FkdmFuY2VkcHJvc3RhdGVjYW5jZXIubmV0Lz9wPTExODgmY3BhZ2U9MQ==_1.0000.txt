			<h1 id="post-1188">On the Horizon- Another New Vaccine, Prostvac</h1>
			By <span class="meta-author"><a href="http://advancedprostatecancer.net/?author=1" title="Posts by Joel">Joel</a></span> 
		</div>
	</div>

	<!--post text with the read more link-->
	<p>Vaccine therapy is one of the new frontiers in cancer research and hopefully, in the very near future, in prostate cancer treatment.  Provenge, about which I have written extensively, will hopefully be approved by the FDA in the next few months.  Now, a potential new vaccine that that is based on a relative of the smallpox virus seems to also offer a life extending ability to men with advanced prostate cancer.  </p>
<p>This new treatment vaccine, called Prostvac-VF, is being developed by BN ImmunoTherapeutics, a division of Danish biotech firm Bavarian Nordic (BAVA.CO).</p>
<p>Preliminary tests conducted on 125 men with advanced prostate cancer that was hormone refractory showed they lived more than 8 months longer than men not treated with the vaccine, said Dr. Philip Kantoff of the Dana-Farber Cancer Institute and Harvard Medical School in Boston, who helped lead the study.<span id="more-1188"></span></p>
<p>“The average survival for these men is two years,” Kantoff said in a telephone interview. “At three years, 30 percent of the men who got the vaccine were still alive.  The exciting news is that Dr Kantoff  indicated a larger study with more men was being planned for later this year.</p>
<p>The study, reported in part at several cancer meetings over the past few months, is detailed in the current Journal of Clinical Oncology.</p>
<p>Both Provenge and Prostvac are so-called therapeutic vaccines, because they treat a disease as opposed to traditional vaccines that prevent disease.    Provenge uses a man’s own immune system cells by attaching an antigen to them in a manufacturing type process and then re-infusing them.  However, Prostvac takes a different approach then Provenge.  Prostvac is a “cell-based vaccine,” according to Kanthoff.  Prostvac utilizes a virus which is related to the small pox virus, the cow pox virus, that is genetically engineered. </p>
<p>The  cowpox virus forms the basis of the current smallpox vaccine and a bird virus called fowlpox.  The cowpox virus is genetically engineered to carry prostate specific antigen (PSA), which is made only by prostate cells. Since prostate cancer tumors make excess amounts of PSA, the vaccine is designed to focus the immune system on these out-of-control tumor cells.</p>
<p>Kanthoff, who has worked on both vaccines, Provenge and Prostvac, said he is not sure which vaccine works better. “It’s just exciting to think that you can alter the immune system,” he said. “To me it is not one versus the other. Both companies are rejoicing in the fact this might work, and the whole field is rejoicing.”</p>
<p>Joel T Nowak MA, MSW</p>
