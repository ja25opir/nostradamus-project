				<div class="post-content">
	  			<p><span style="color: #000000;"><span style="color: #ff6600;"><strong>For this week”s meditation,</strong></span> we turn to American Zen master <strong>Alan Watts</strong>, who wrote…</span></p>
<blockquote><p><span style="color: #000000;">I have realized that the past and the future are real illusions, that they exist only in the present, which is what there is and all there is.</span></p></blockquote>
<p><span style="color: #000000;"><span style="color: #000000;">Oh, so true.</span></span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Join me in the present moment, won’t you?</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Take a long, slow deep breath, hold for a second, and then exhale slowly. Repeat several times until you feel your consciousness shift into the moment.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">There.</span><br>
<span style="color: #000000;"><br>
That’s it.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">That’s all there is.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">It’s more than enough, too, isn’t it?</span></p>
