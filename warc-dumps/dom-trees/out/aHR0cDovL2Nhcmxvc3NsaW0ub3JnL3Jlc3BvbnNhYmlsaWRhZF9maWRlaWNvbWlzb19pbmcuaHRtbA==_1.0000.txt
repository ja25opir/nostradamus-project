<div id="contenido">
  <p><strong>Eng. Carlos Slim participated in the opening ceremony of the event <em>Fideicomiso para el Impulso de la Ingeniería</em> organized by the <em>Colegio de Ingenieros  Civiles de México</em> (Civil Engineers College) in which the new web site  iNGENET was introduced,&nbsp; created and  sponsored by TELMEX.<br>
  </strong>Mexico City, November 20th, 2008. 
  
  </p><p>During his presentation, Eng. Slim expressed that “the best way to cope  with poverty is employment, health and education. The better education, the  better opportunities of jobs in the future”.</p>
  <p>And added “it must be a priority to invest in order to urge the  employment; it is more important to think how we can generate more employment  than be focused in the economic growth, we need to set in motion small and  medium-size business, attend the agricultural sector, create infrastructure and  concentrate in housing projects”.</p>
  <p>Furthermore, Eng. Slim stated that it is necessary to reactivate credits  around the world and affirmed that one of the biggest problems to prevent is  the worldwide credit restriction.</p>
  <p>The <em>Fideicomiso para el Impulso de  la Ingeniería</em> is trying to support the infrastructure of the country, it will cost 200  million pesos in which 51% of this resources will be a contribution of&nbsp; Mexican construction firms and the other 49  percent will be donated by the <em>Fondo  Nacional de Infraestructura</em> (Infrastructure National Fund) of the  government of Mexico.</p>
  <p>iNGENET (<a href="http://www.ingenet.com.mx">www.ingenet.com.mx</a>) is  a web site developed by TELMEX and it is focused to promote de Engineer sector,  as well as the infrastructure.&nbsp; It’s a  non-profit program for engineers, students, firms and all the ones interested  in this sector.&nbsp; It will include  infrastructure and engineer themes with the collaboration of Private  Institutions, the Public Sector, Academic Institutions and professional  Colleges.<br>
  </p>
