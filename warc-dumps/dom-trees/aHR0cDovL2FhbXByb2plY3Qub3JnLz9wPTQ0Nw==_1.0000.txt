			<!-- google_ad_section_start --><p><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-448" title="bp oil spill" src="http://aamproject.org/wp-content/uploads/2010/07/bp-oil-spill-300x218.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="218">Workers on the relief wells being drilled to stop the leak in the Gulf of Mexico have halted operations for further analysis of the operation before proceeding.&nbsp; According to Thad Allen, in charge of the capping and clean up effort, the well may benefit from additional analysis before proceeding.&nbsp; The halt is only expected to last for 24 to 48 hours and the team should know around mid-day today when they will resume drilling of the relief well.</p>
<p>This news on top of a new cap that’s been placed over the main leak, which seems to be doing a good job of keeping the majority of the oil from escaping the well.&nbsp; At this point, it seems as though the crews working on the disaster want to ensure that everything is done correctly, that there’s no additional problems in the process or resultant from the effort and that when the job is done it won’t pose a risk to the environment due to possible leaking or rupture in the future.</p>
<p>For now, the citizens of the gulf coast are mainly relieved that it looks as though the spilling is nearing an end.&nbsp; They still have years of clean-up and recuperation before the issue goes away, but this long-awaited first step is promising for those who are out of work and whose lives have been irrevocably altered.</p><div class="addtoany_share_save_container"><ul class="addtoany_list"><li><a class="a2a_dd addtoany_share_save" href="http://www.addtoany.com/share_save"><img src="http://aamproject.org/wp-content/plugins/add-to-any/share_save_171_16.png" width="171" height="16" alt="Share/Bookmark"></a></li></ul>
