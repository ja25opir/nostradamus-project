<blockquote><p>From What We’ve Seen: Are we actually looking forward to <em>Three Rivers</em>? I’m not so sure, but it didn’t seem fair to leave CBS out of this list entirely, so I cut yet another ABC show that I wasn’t really *all* that excited for (<em>Fast Forward</em>) and added <em>Three Rivers</em>, because… well.. I know <strong>some</strong> people are looking forward to Alex O’Loughlin’s return to primetime with the white-hot passion of at least 20 or 30 suns. O’Loughlin looks suitably cast as the bad-boy surgeon-with-a-soul, but in the clips, he’s frequently upstaged by “The L Word” veteran Katherine Moennig. The rest of the cast, including <em>Wolverine</em> alum Daniel Henney, Justina Muchado and Julia Ormond, is also solid. The presentation clip was surprisingly emotional and melodramatic, which isn’t necessarily CBS’ stock-in-trade, so we’ll see how they do with this show.</p></blockquote>

		<div class="post-info">
				<span class="date time published" title="2009-05-21T08:24:22+0000">May 21, 2009</span>  By <span class="author vcard"><a href="http://alexoloughlinrocks.com/author/MizO-2/" title="Posts by mizzoh" rel="nofollow">mizzoh</a></span> <span class="post-comments"><a href="http://alexoloughlinrocks.com/alexoloughlin/pittsburgh-post-gazette-three-rivers-runs-through-it#comments" rel="nofollow">2 Comments</a></span>

		<div class="entry-content">
			<p><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-3036" style="margin: 5px 10px;" title="pittsburgh-post-gazette21may09" src="http://alexoloughlinrocks.com/wp-content/uploads/pittsburgh-post-gazette21may09-300x300.jpg" alt="pittsburgh-post-gazette21may09" width="300" height="300"><em>Rob Owen, TV editor at the Pittsburgh Post-Gazette,&nbsp; published an article today introducing the lead characters from </em><em>Three Rivers as published in <a title="Three Rivers Synopsis" href="http://alexoloughlinrocks.com/latest-headline/three-rivers-synopsis" target="_blank">the synopsis</a> and provided some interesting background information on the pilot that was shot in Pittsburgh in March.&nbsp; Rob will be posting more on Alex O’Loughlin’s new TV show in the future, so keep an eye out for his <a title="Tuned In Journal Blog" href="http://community.post-gazette.com/blogs/tunedin/default.aspx" target="_blank">blog updates</a>.</em></p>
<p><strong>Tuned In: CBS Schedule – ‘Three Rivers’ Runs Through it</strong></p>
<p>Thursday, May 21<br>
<em>by Rob Owen, Pittsburgh Post-Gazette</em></p>
<p>Five years after CBS canceled its last Pittsburgh-set series, “The Guardian,” the network returns to the ‘Burgh for the aptly titled “Three Rivers,” a medical drama that will air at 9 p.m. Sundays beginning this fall.</p>
<p>“Three Rivers” is set in the world of transplant surgery among organ donors, recipients and surgeons at a Pittsburgh hospital.</p>
<p>The “Three Rivers” pilot filmed in Pittsburgh in late March and early April using the closed Brownsville Tri-County Hospital as its primary location. Other locations included, but were not limited to, Allegheny County Airport, the David L. Lawrence Convention Center and Duquesne University’s A.J. Palumbo Center, where members of the Point Park men’s basketball team filmed a scene involving a player who faces a medical emergency during a practice session.</p>
<div id="TixyyLink" style="border: medium none; overflow: hidden; color: #000000; background-color: transparent; text-align: left; text-decoration: none;">
<p>A CBS publicist did not know where subsequent episodes of the series will film, but the Pittsburgh Film Office has gotten no confirmation that “Three Rivers” is returning to Pittsburgh to film additional episodes. A likely scenario is that the series will shoot primarily on the West Coast, like “The Guardian,” returning to Pittsburgh on occasion for pick-up shots. Although Pennsylvania tax incentives make filming locally attractive to filmmakers, actors often have contracts that limit series filming locations to film industry centers (e.g. Los Angeles, Vancouver, New York).</p>
