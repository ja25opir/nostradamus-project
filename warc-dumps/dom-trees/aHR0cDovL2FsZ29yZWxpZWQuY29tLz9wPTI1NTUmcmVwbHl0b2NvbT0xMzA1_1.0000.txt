				<span class="author">By Klockarman</span>				<span class="category"><a href="http://algorelied.com/?cat=57" title="View all posts in Global cooling" rel="category">Global cooling</a>,  <a href="http://algorelied.com/?cat=20" title="View all posts in Letter to the Editor" rel="category">Letter to the Editor</a>,  <a href="http://algorelied.com/?cat=226" title="View all posts in global warming" rel="category">global warming</a></span>

          <div class="entry fix">
            <p>This is getting ridiculous.</p>
<p>Lately it’s been called “<a href="http://www.grist.org/article/2009-06-11-ecoamerica-public-opinion/" target="_blank">our deteriorating atmosphere</a>“.&nbsp; Before that it was the “climate crisis”.&nbsp; Before that it was called “climate change”.&nbsp; Before that it had been called “global warming”.&nbsp; Before that it had been called the weather.&nbsp; Now, Joy Trueblood, of Sandy, Oregon proposes it be called, “man-made atmosphere change”. No doubt because the earth hasn’t been warming for the past eleven years.</p>
<p><a href="http://blog.oregonlive.com/myoregon/2009/07/letters_crater_lake_flights_ca.html" target="_blank">From The Oregonian</a>:</p>
<blockquote><p>The recent letters in your paper disputing climate change show clearly why we need to relabel the problem as “man-made atmosphere change.”</p>
<p>Man-made atmosphere change is real. It’s happening now. It can’t be disputed. It can be measured. All reputable scientists know it’s happening, and most laymen can understand it.</p>
<p>It can be shown in pictures of smokestacks and tailpipes emitting carbon dioxide along with other pollutants. Pictures make it easier for climate change deniers to understand what is happening.</p>
<p>Climate change is just one consequence of letting polluters mess with our atmosphere. It seems incredibly stupid, as well as morally wrong, to change the atmosphere that produced and sustains our life on Earth, especially if solutions are as simple as more emissions controls on smokestacks and cars, driving less, eating less beef and using birth control.</p>
<p>JOY TRUEBLOOD<br>
Sandy</p></blockquote>

Water vapor makes up 95 percent of the greenhouse gases.  And greenhouse gases keep our planet from being frozen wasteland.  They are necessary for us to live.</p>
<p>You’ve been sold a bill of goods based on a false belief. Educate yourself.</p>
<p>You’ve been conned.</p>
