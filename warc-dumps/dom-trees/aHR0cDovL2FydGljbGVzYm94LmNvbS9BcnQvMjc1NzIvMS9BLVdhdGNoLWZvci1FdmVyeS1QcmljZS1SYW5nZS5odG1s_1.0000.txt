<p>Want to buy a watch but don’t know where to start? Maybe the best way to narrow down your choices is by looking at the price. Here is a list of watches for all price ranges. Many of these watch manufacturers make watches that can appear in more than one category, but for the sake of simplicity and organization, I have listed them according to their average watch price.</p><p>Low Range: $10-350: These watches are stylish and affordable. If you aren’t burdened by a tight budget, you can even buy two! One leather and one stainless steel will allow you all the variety you’ll need. Fossil, Casio, Invicta, Mondaine, Skagen, Penguin,Tissot, Kenneth Cole, Swatch, Nixon, Diesel, Guess, J.Crew</p><p>The Middle Range $350-$3,500: These watches are for the more serious horologist. Wearing one of these means that making a statement is important to you, and sacrificing style is a no-no.</p><p>Swiss: Maurice Lacroix, Oris, Movado, Tissot, Fortis, Tag Heuer, Longines, Rado, Edox, Swiss Army, Frederique Constant, Raymond Weil.</p><p>French: Tutima, Muhle Glashutte, Nivrel, Sinn, Nomos Glashutte and Limes</p><p>Italian: Brera Orologi, Officina Del Tempo, Momo Design and U-Boat</p><p>Japanese: Seiko, Citizen, Oceanus</p><p>American: Hamilton, Concord, Accutron, Ball, Bathys, Invicta, Luminox and Bulova</p><p>The High Range: $3,500 - $10,000 This range is for the true collector. If you are spending thousands of dollars on a watch, you can look at it almost like an investment, because these pieces of craftsmanship certainly do appreciate with time. Moreover, when you pay the most, you buy the best, and these watches will reflect your fine taste:</p><p>Patek Philippe, Bvlgari, Panerai, A Lange Watches, Audemars Piguet, Rolex, Omega, Baume &amp; Mercier, JeanRichard, Breitling, IWC, Montblanc, de Grisogono, Chopard, Girard-Perregaux, Greubel Forsey, Vacheron Constantin, Breguet, Jacob &amp; Co., Hublot, Ulysse Nardin, Blancpain, Parmigiani Fleurier, Franck Muller, Piaget</p><p>Wearing a watch to tell the time is convenient, but let’s face it, if you really want to know the time, you can look at your cell phone. Watch wearers love their timepieces because of how it makes them feel, like there are things to be done, and like they’re the person to do them. While there are even more brands than listed here, many of them use jewelry to transform a well manufactured watch into a gilded masterpiece. Other factors that can help you decide what kind of watch you want are how often you will wear it, and where. Do you want a timepiece for the office? For a night out on the town? For everyday use? For extreme activities? Lastly, consider what kind of maintenance the watch will require, and whether you will ever buy another watch in the future. Perhaps it’s worth it to you to save up for the kind of watch you pass down to your grandchildren. Or maybe you want to begin a collection of watches. Depending on where you find yourself in life, there are many good watches for everyone; it’s just a matter of what features you want, and how much you’re willing to spend.<br></p><!-- google_ad_section_end --></td>
