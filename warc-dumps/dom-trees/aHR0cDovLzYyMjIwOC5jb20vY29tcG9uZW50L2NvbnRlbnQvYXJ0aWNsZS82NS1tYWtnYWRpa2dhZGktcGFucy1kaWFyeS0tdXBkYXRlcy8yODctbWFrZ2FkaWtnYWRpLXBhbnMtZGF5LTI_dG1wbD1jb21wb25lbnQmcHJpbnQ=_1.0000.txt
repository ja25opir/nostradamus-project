The mouse quickly ran and jumped onto the groundsheet again and disappeared into the folds. It is a massive groundsheet so I didn’t want to unfold it completely to look for the mouse so I just shook it very well.</p><p>The first 10km of the day was again a struggle in the wet and salty clay and took a lot of energy to get through, but after that the pan surface gradually improved and got harder and dryer. I could see Khubu on the horizon to I had something to aim for and I put my head down and walked as hard as I could.&nbsp; In the last 10km I saw hundreds of eggs. These are flamingo eggs and at one stage I could count over 50 big eggs without moving. <br>I only managed to get to the island at 11:35 and by that time I was baking in the sun. I headed straight for the first big tree and took only the food bag and water with me and left my trailer at the edge of the island.&nbsp; As I grabbed the food bag I saw that the mouse from earlier the morning was still in between my things. Shame the little guy was hitching a ride and was now 20km away from home.</p><p>Only at 3:30pm did I dare go back into the sun to look for a nice spot somewhere around the island to camp. On the one side of the island there is a campsite but I didn’t want to camp between all the other tourists and get asked about the trailer all the time. When people see the trailer they want to know what the hell I’m up to and I just wanted to be left alone. One of the locals that help to run the campsite came past me later that evening on his bicycle when I was busy eating and I called him to stop. I wanted to get some information from him about the route I was planning to follow and about these dangerous wild animals I was stressing about.</p><p></p><div class="slideshow cut">

In my initial planning I wanted to use this track but all the pictures that I could find that had this track on looked to me as if the sand would be too soft which makes pulling the trailer very difficult. He said the track is only soft in very few sections and would recommend I take it. He also said that I should not worry about wild animals unless I go very close to the Makgadikgadi Nature Reserve which is on the west edge of the Ntwetwe Pan.&nbsp; The section of bush where I will walk tomorrow is all used for cattle farming and there would be no dangerous wild animals. Yipee.</p><p>Khubu Island is a magical place. There are some of the biggest Baobab trees I’ve ever seen in my live and you can climb on some huge rocks to get a great view of the pans. I would definitely go back there again in the future.</p><p>&nbsp;</p><p><a href="/images/stories/makgadikgadi/panos/khubu_pano_big.jpg" title="View South from Khubu Island" rel="lightbox"><img src="/images/stories/makgadikgadi/panos/khubu_pano_small.jpg" border="0" alt="View South from Khubu Island" title="View South from Khubu Island" width="640"></a></p><p><strong>View south (where I came from) from Khubu Island - Click to Enlarge</strong></p>
