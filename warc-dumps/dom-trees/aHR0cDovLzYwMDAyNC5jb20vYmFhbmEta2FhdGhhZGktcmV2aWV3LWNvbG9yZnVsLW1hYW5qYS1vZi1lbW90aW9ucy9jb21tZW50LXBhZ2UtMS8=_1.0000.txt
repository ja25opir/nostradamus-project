        Baana Kaathadi Review – Colorful Maanja of Emotions 
          
            <a href="http://600024.com/ta/baana-kaathadi-review-colorful-maanja-of-emotions/comment-page-1/"><img src="http://en.600024.com/images/web/toreadtamil.jpg"></a>
            </h2>                     
	<div class="break"></div>
    <p class="info">
        Posted at August 7th, 2010 under <a href="http://600024.com/review/movie-review/" title="View all posts in Movie Review" rel="category tag">Movie Review</a>, <a href="http://600024.com/review/" title="View all posts in Review" rel="category tag">Review</a> |   <a href="http://600024.com/baana-kaathadi-review-colorful-maanja-of-emotions/#comments" title="Comment on Baana Kaathadi Review – Colorful Maanja of Emotions">4 Comments »</a>                                               
    </p>

<li><a href="http://twitter.com/share" class="twitter-share-button" data-count="horizontal" data-via="600024">Tweet</a><script type="text/javascript" src="http://platform.twitter.com/widgets.js"></script></li><li><a name="fb_share" type="button_count" href="http://www.facebook.com/sharer.php">Share</a><script src="http://static.ak.fbcdn.net/connect.php/js/FB.Share" type="text/javascript"></script></li><li><a title="Post to Google Buzz" class="google-buzz-button" href="http://www.google.com/buzz/post" data-button-style="small-count"></a><script type="text/javascript" src="http://www.google.com/buzz/api/button.js"></script></li></ul><p>Five ‘Boys’ in a ‘Colony’ in ‘Chennai’ and a ‘high class lass’ is the ‘Baana Kaathadi’, produced by ‘Senthil Thyagarajan’ and ‘T Arjun’ for ‘Sathya Jyothi Films’. Screenplay is a tautly stretched rope which the director treads and any overbalance in the itinerary is a spoilt show. Fortunately, director ‘Badri Venkatesh’ who has also written the screenplay can shroud the interim slips. It is human sensibilities, the right doze of humor, the one-liners and the native slang realistically uttered by all the characters, without any tentativeness by some of the seasoned actors that do the rescue.</p>
<p>‘Badri Venkatesh’s screenplay takes a few of the realistic, offbeat, interesting moments such as the rubbing of the hands on the ground before boarding the bus. He lets you wait and guess (including what the story is, till the end), and then breaks the precognition of the audience by the fourth or the fifth possible ramifications ‘unfetchable’ by the human horizons. Badri also leaves most of the scenes early to proceed to the next or perhaps it is the editing of Suresh Urs. All these tend to make interesting moments of titillation but fail to concoct the necessary orientation.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://600024.com/files/2010/08/baanakaathadi_2.jpg"><img title="baanakaathadi_2" src="http://600024.com/files/2010/08/baanakaathadi_2.jpg" alt="" width="600"></a></strong></p>    
    
<p>The story written by ‘Lakshmikanthan’ is a concocted dish with all the spice, pungency, sweetness and sourness. The central theme of the film, ‘Kites,’ is a mere backdrop and the characters take the foreground. At the end of the day, you love the characters, their raw local qualities and their living through the entire film. The best scene of the film is in the stark realism equivalent to the ‘City of God’ in the fight for the kite (Baana Kaathadi) between the lead, his friends, another and his friends.</p>
<p>‘Prasanna’ is an actor with immense potential, ‘well-possessed’ maturity and stellar execution who can pull off any role with ease. It is a pity that a raw Tamil actor as Prasanna is left out in most films for borrowing actors from other places aside of the demarcation. Prasanna proves that he is majestic and is a better actor than any other of the supporting actors.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://600024.com/files/2010/08/baanakaathadi_5.jpg"><img title="baanakaathadi_5" src="http://600024.com/files/2010/08/baanakaathadi_5.jpg" alt="" width="600"></a></strong></p>
<p>‘Samantha’ is the only weak link who is a doll by her looks but needs severe indenturing at least in the ‘Photon Kathaas’ or Goutham Menon’s factory. There is many a scene for her to score but her effusions are expressed through her impassiveness. ‘Yuvan Shankar Raja’s ability to sense films that work and films that don’t is evident through his music and songs for ‘Baana Kaathadi’. He does seem to be involved himself in the joyous badinages, natters, antics and flying of Kites. He scores his heart out in such scenes but reminds you of ‘Manmadhan’ in the love scenes, the pangs of separation and the reunion.</p>
<p>‘Richard M Nathan’s Cinematography was expecting scenes to make the cinematography stand out in the script which is vastly dominated by the characters and their faces. The Cinematographer makes the right use of the two or three opportunities he was given – ‘Prasanna’s sequences of crime, the fight for the kites, the smaller than normal dwelling of ‘Atharva’, the songs and the reproduction of the hero’s strong points in his looks.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://600024.com/files/2010/08/baanakaathadi_3.jpg"><img title="baanakaathadi_3" src="http://600024.com/files/2010/08/baanakaathadi_3.jpg" alt="" width="600"></a></strong></p>
<p>‘Mounika’ is used to Chennai and she has often been criticized for her overdoing roles but has scaled down a little to add to the realism. She is a heart stealer as ‘Ramesh’s (Atharva’s) mother while Prasanna is a show stealer as ‘Maanja Ravi’. ‘Karunas’ is pivotal though he does a little of a cringe-making humor attempts with enough of repetition leading to three to four boring repeat of a sequence with his father ‘TP Gajendran’.</p>
<p>All throughout, the character of ‘Atharva’ is squeaky-clean, innocent, fragile and vulnerable. The vulnerability of Atharva is utilized by the director to keep the soaring tension on his ‘life’ alive when Prasanna is pitted against Atharva by his boss ‘Naan Kadavul Rajendran’. Prasanna has a soft corner for Atharva but even then the fear grips the audience. The lead character and the audience’s concerns are put to utmost tension through an item song performed by ‘Debi Dutta’, the succeeding scenes and the climax.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://600024.com/files/2010/08/baanakaathadi_4.jpg"><img title="baanakaathadi_4" src="http://600024.com/files/2010/08/baanakaathadi_4.jpg" alt="" width="600"></a></strong></p>
<p>All the other supporting cast, the friends of Atharva and the henchmen of the outlaw Prasanna have done a good job except for the heroine Samantha’s friend who is awkward with her acting. Finally, the promising debutant Atharva! Atharva was prepared for the purpose under the tutelages of his father ‘Murali’, of ‘Idhayam’ fame, who does a subtle yet hilarious spoof of a cameo of himself in the film.</p>
<p>The offspring shows a stylish body language, well-built upper anatomy and the face of a ‘chocolate boy hero’, perhaps a little odd for the character. What more can you expect of a debutant who is given a start to his career with the native qualities that he was brought up with? He could do the most of it with considerable ease as he must have been used to it or must have at least known about.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://600024.com/files/2010/08/baanakaathadi_1.jpg"><img title="baanakaathadi_1" src="http://600024.com/files/2010/08/baanakaathadi_1.jpg" alt="" width="600"></a></strong></p>
<p>Despite his physical appearance well suited for an ‘upper-middle-class-metropolitan-lad’, he essays the role with understanding which makes him recognizable as promising to succeed in the future. He will find out that he has to emote more in intense emotional scenes. Atharva slightly surpasses the median in his very first film.</p>
<p><strong>Positives</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Prasanna</li>
<li>Screenplay</li>
<li>Some nice, interesting and entertaining moments</li>
<li>Yuvan Shankar Raja</li>
<li>Atharva &amp; Samantha (Appearance) <img src="http://600024.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif" alt=":)" class="wp-smiley"> </li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Negatives</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Samantha (Acting)</li>
<li>Lack of orientation of the Script</li>
<li>Lack of story</li>
<li>Misleading and underutilized main theme (Kites)</li>
<li>Climax</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Verdict</strong></p>
<p>Purely enjoyable for its raw native qualities and the characters, Baana Kaathadi, produced by Senthil Thyagarajan and T Arjun for Sathya Jyothi Films and directed by Badri Venkatesh, has some quirky moments of deception that makes your heart go out for the lead character. Although the climax tells upon the film and at times the film sinks into the regular and the usual, the stark realism in the setting is familiar for the lay audience and the natives.</p>
<p>Adding to this, Atharva has something to perform though not in a much hyped setting for an opening as that of ‘Silambarasan’ and ‘Dhanush’. Baana Kaathadi steals your heart in patches and stands apart from the regular. Baana Kaathadi is a better film of the lots being unlike the usual commercial films though the attempt has taken some misled direction. The characters lovable – the film enjoyable!</p>
