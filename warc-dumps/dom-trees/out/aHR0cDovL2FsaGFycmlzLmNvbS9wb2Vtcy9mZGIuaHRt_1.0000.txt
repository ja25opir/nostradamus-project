<a name="TheWater"><h4>The Water</h4></a> 
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
You cry your first in your mother's arms.<br>
The water trickles down the drain.<br>
<br>
You soon grow into a toddler's knowing.<br>
The water flows beneath the streets.<br>
<br>
You attend your schools for diplomas, degrees.<br>
The water enters a nearby stream.<br>
<br>
You have your wedding, children, career.<br>
The water joins a seaward-flowing river.<br>
<br>
You make mistakes in ethics; health goes weak.<br>
The water reaches the peace of the sea.<br>
<br>
You retire from your career to savor life.<br>
The water now is one with all the seas.<br>
<br>
You suffer through precursors of mortality.<br>
The water feels a need to rise.<br>
<br>
Your body quits, and you leave it where it is.<br>
The water rises through a mist into a cloud.<br>
<br>
You enjoy long bliss in the space of Light.<br>
The water joins a darkening cloud.<br>
<br>
You feel a longing toward the physical again.<br>
The water rains down and seeps into a well.<br>
<br>
Your vision of the Light has faded now.<br>
The water is drawn from the well for drinking.<br>
<br>
You feel confined and utterly doomed.<br>
The water breaks.<br>
<br>
You cry your first in your mother's arms.<br>
The water trickles down the drain.<br>
</font>
</td></tr></tbody></table>

<a name="JanuaryAdagio"><h4>January Adagio</h4></a> 
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
Tonight at 10:30 I went out<br>
for my walk.  In the distance<br>
I heard a major commotion<br>
of geese.  At first I thought<br>
a flock might fly overhead,<br>
though the hour was far too late<br>
for geese to be aloft.<br>
<br>
But the sound wasn't moving.<br>
<br>
I heard a train's rumble,<br>
then its mournful horn.<br>
A freight was crossing<br>
the railroad bridge<br>
over the Fox River<br>
close to where the geese<br>
were overnighting.<br>
<br>
As I turned around toward home<br>
I still could hear them fret and scold<br>
in chaotic counterpoint with<br>
the diesel's basso continuo.<br>
<br>
And the stars tonight burned<br>
bright holes in the sky, decorating<br>
bare tree branches overhead<br>
like lingering holiday lighting.<br>
<br>
After the train had rumbled off<br>
to where nocturnal trains all go,<br>
the neighborhood assumed a hush<br>
perturbed only by my footsteps.<br>
<br>
Hardly anything is quieter<br>
than distant sleeping geese<br>
and star-bespeckled trees.<br>
</font>
</td></tr></tbody></table>

<a name="KarmaYoga"><h4>Karma Yoga</h4></a> 
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
Living every hour<br>
in the exact middle<br>
of my weaknesses,<br>
I work some more.<br>
<br>
Knowing the ways<br>
I fell apart before<br>
and took poor paths,<br>
I work some more.<br>
<br>
To piece together<br>
my fragmentary<br>
feelings for peace,<br>
I work some more.<br>
<br>
Pretty sure I will<br>
later fail to restrain<br>
some urges within me,<br>
I work some more.<br>
<br>
When all of my jobs<br>
on earth are done and<br>
I'm in and out of heaven,<br>
I will work some more.<br>
</font>
</td></tr></tbody></table>

<a name="BirdOmens"><h4>Bird Omens</h4></a> 
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
When you go for a walk<br>
in your nearby forest,<br>
you see pairs of cardinals<br>
and thrill to their singing.<br>
<br>
One time you overheard<br>
two owls conversing<br>
between bare trees.<br>
<br>
In summer you have<br>
stared breathless<br>
at a heron standing<br>
Samadhi-like<br>
beside your lake.<br>
<br>
Birds of beauty<br>
want to be near you.<br>
Your heart flies up<br>
with these fliers<br>
and knows into<br>
their knowing.<br>
<br>
Today as I walked<br>
across an open field,<br>
hundreds of crows<br>
flew overhead,<br>
snidely cawing from<br>
confusing clouds<br>
of cacophony.<br>
<br>
After they were gone,<br>
I walked on in silence<br>
and knew nothing.<br>
</font>
</td></tr></tbody></table>

<h4>Farmer Karma</h4> 
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
I was a boy farmer<br>
because I had to be<br>
because my father<br>
was a man farmer<br>
and all my granddads<br>
back to almost Adam<br>
had been boy farmers<br>
and man farmers<br>
and that was that.<br>
I hardly even realized<br>
that I hated farming<br>
but just did it because<br>
and forever because.<br>
<br>
I learned how to<br>
sharpen a hoe<br>
and cut through my<br>
hot-day reluctance<br>
in order to kill Canadian<br>
thistles in mechanical<br>
planticide.  Dad told<br>
me that the county<br>
thistle warden might<br>
assess us a fine if we<br>
had too many thistles.<br>
Chop, chop, chop,<br>
I spiraled into each patch<br>
and then on to the next,<br>
never finishing them all.<br>
<br>
I learned how to start<br>
the John Deere Model A<br>
tractor by yanking<br>
the top of its flywheel<br>
mightily to the left<br>
with the petcocks open<br>
to reduce compression<br>
until things got to popping<br>
then closing the petcocks<br>
for more power.<br>
That Model A and I were<br>
partners who bounced<br>
across years of bumpy soil<br>
pulling a drag or a disk<br>
or a 3-bottom plow.<br>
High in the bucket seat,<br>
teeth into the gritty air,<br>
I was as much a slave<br>
to the A as it to me,<br>
as much a slave<br>
to the farm<br>
as any farmer is.<br>
<br>
I shoveled grain<br>
inside bins where<br>
dust polluted the air<br>
and filled my lungs<br>
so full that<br>
a time or two<br>
I almost died<br>
from asthma.<br>
But dying would be<br>
a slacker's excuse,<br>
and the grain had<br>
to be leveled.<br>
<br>
In the haymow<br>
there was also,<br>
guess what,<br>
dust and heat<br>
enough to turn<br>
my lungs into<br>
solid protoplasm--<br>
what bronchial tubes?<br>
When older, I got to stay<br>
outside and throw<br>
the bales onto<br>
the Mayrath hay<br>
elevator and breathe<br>
the same good air that<br>
our cows all breathed.<br>
<br>
I was always dutiful.<br>
I never gave Dad<br>
a single hint that<br>
I didn't like farming.<br>
No hint, that is,<br>
other than my stoic<br>
attitude, my yes-boss<br>
obedience, my lack<br>
of any initiative,<br>
and my slipshod work.<br>
These failings didn't matter<br>
because there was the farm<br>
and there were we<br>
and the earth was turning<br>
and the weather was erratic<br>
and new work grew up<br>
as fast as the precious corn.<br>
<br>
Dad never tried to teach<br>
me anything technical<br>
about how to farm.<br>
He could see my soul.<br>
One look at me<br>
on any day of any week<br>
told him that this boy<br>
would never be a farmer.<br>
No point in telling the boy<br>
how best to rotate crops<br>
or how to repair a combine<br>
or how to choose fertilizer<br>
or when to sell the grain.<br>
Such breath would<br>
have been as wasted<br>
as a cold March wind<br>
across the railroad field.<br>
<br>
Dad was a good farmer<br>
and a good man.<br>
Farming is good, too.<br>
We get to eat from it.<br>
But farming gets glorified<br>
pretty often, and I never<br>
partook of that schmaltz.<br>
<br>
I was a tractor driver<br>
who would watch train<br>
after train go by<br>
on the Burlington<br>
and wave at the engineers<br>
and caboosemen,<br>
all of us dutifully chained<br>
to our turning wheels.<br>
<br>
I was a manure pitcher<br>
and a manure spreader<br>
who knew the cows had<br>
to produce this but didn't<br>
see my future in it.<br>
<br>
Farmer karma was<br>
my inherited destiny<br>
until college days<br>
when I learned how<br>
to be amply engrossed<br>
in motions of the mind<br>
and never later hankered<br>
for any life on any farm.<br>
</font>
</td></tr></tbody></table>

<a name="ThankYou"><h4>Thank You</h4></a> 
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
Thank most you<br>
for all little things big.<br>
<br>
Beams of kindness<br>
illumine all paths of you<br>
<br>
and I am days on end<br>
in your gentle debt.<br>
<br>
Accept please this<br>
as my up payment.<br>
</font>

<a name="NightLight"><h4>Night Light</h4></a> 
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
Melancholy needs a walk,<br>
so out I carry it at 11 p.m.<br>
to study two universes,<br>
out and in.<br>
<br>
Our neighborhood is dotted with<br>
random porch and yard lamps<br>
lighting the way for nobody<br>
and me.<br>
<br>
An hour above setting in the west,<br>
our less-than-first-quarter moon<br>
smiles inscrutably like a queen<br>
in state.<br>
<br>
Gliding through the trees, she<br>
offers only used rays to my heart,<br>
but light being now difficult to find,<br>
I accept.<br>
<br>
With far-away stars shining only because<br>
they must, above a neighborhood where<br>
yard lamps are glowing, thanks to<br>
owners,<br>
<br>
a breath now washes through my chest<br>
inviting me to turn my melancholy<br>
over to night's infinite matrix of Beings<br>
who shine.<br>
<br>
I do, and return home with lungs full<br>
of light from outer and inner space,<br>
and from yard lamps left on for all<br>
who walk.<br>
</font>
</td></tr></tbody></table>

<a name="ManWalking"><h4>Man Walking</h4></a> 
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
There is a man<br>
walking behind me<br>
on Wood Street<br>
in Chicago.<br>
<br>
He can't know<br>
my heart hums<br>
a surging theme<br>
from Movement 1<br>
of Mahler's Tenth.<br>
<br>
He can't know<br>
why I am walking<br>
on Wood Street<br>
in Chicago.<br>
<br>
And why am I?<br>
It takes too long<br>
to think about.<br>
<br>
Who is this man<br>
behind me,<br>
walking?<br>
<br>
What flavors<br>
his feelings?<br>
What obstacles<br>
has he overcome?<br>
What song<br>
is in him?<br>
<br>
I somehow am<br>
this man walking<br>
on Wood Street<br>
in Chicago.<br>
<br>
I am<br>
his walkingness<br>
behind me,<br>
his grapplingness<br>
with his day.<br>
<br>
I can only know<br>
my own form<br>
but he and I<br>
are breathing of<br>
the same Breath.<br>
<br>
Mahler's Tenth<br>
plays on within me<br>
as I enter a building.<br>
<br>
The man continues<br>
along the street<br>
paying absolutely<br>
no attention to me,<br>
<br>
this man walking<br>
on Wood Street<br>
in Chicago<br>
who I am.<br>
</font>
</td></tr></tbody></table>

<a name="OurFirstWarmDay"><h4>Our First Warm Day</h4></a> 
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
If I were to write about our first warm day of spring,<br>
<br>
I would write about the stuttering<br>
burglar-alarm honks of a car<br>
two blocks away.<br>
<br>
I would write about our waving neighbor<br>
who slowly rides his motorcycle<br>
out into the breeze, seeming to think<br>
nothing of his vulnerability.<br>
<br>
I would write about the silent force<br>
that brings the daffodils to bloom<br>
and emboldens secret romances.<br>
<br>
I would write about children loudly vying<br>
for token goals and supremacies<br>
in outdoor made-up games.<br>
<br>
I would write about the lush air<br>
playing inside my chest in C-major.<br>
<br>
I would write about Celestial Light<br>
beaming upon all and within all<br>
while taken for granted by most.<br>
<br>
I would watch the setting sun,<br>
<br>
listen to the dusk birds,<br>
<br>
watch for the first star,<br>
<br>
pray my drop into the Beneficent Stream<br>
that flows within every person's heart<br>
and every star's,<br>
<br>
then drop into the heights<br>
to write without a pen<br>
upon the folds of Infinity's Cloak<br>
about our first warm day of spring.<br>
</font>
</td></tr></tbody></table>

<a name="World"><h4>World</h4></a> 
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
Is a world hard<br>
like a cue ball?<br>
Or beyond touch?<br>
<br>
Does it jangle<br>
with war threats<br>
or does it hum<br>
soft in the heart<br>
like tuned strings<br>
on a fine harp?<br>
<br>
Is a world separate I's<br>
on a spinning rock<br>
engaged and enraged<br>
with each other<br>
while blinded by what<br>
they can merely see?<br>
<br>
Or is a world precisely<br>
who one can be<br>
(within utmost Who)<br>
subtler than mind<br>
with endless stairs<br>
from love up to Be?<br>
</font>
</td></tr></tbody></table>

<a name="AnInwardEast"><h4>An Inward East</h4></a> 
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
To calm a care or soothe an anger storm<br>
you pause to breathe your vital inside sun<br>
and, richly quiet with its steady glow<br>
of coremost tenderness and flooding peace,<br>
you reinterpret body's aching bones<br>
as levers placed for mystic ministry,<br>
propelled and infinitely smiled upon<br>
by forces which, when tapped, give tenfold strength.<br>
You find your earth eyes lidded from the room<br>
and focused now on lightened higherness.<br>
<br>
In light we are as one, beloved friend.<br>
How can a doubt or fear feel more than mere<br>
when in and up we set our inner sight<br>
to see a splendor further east than east?<br>
</font>
</td></tr></tbody></table>

<a name="Absence"><h4>Absence</h4></a> 
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
I always thought that you,<br>
dear friend, had been away<br>
due to a long, far journey.<br>
<br>
I thought I knew you well,<br>
although I had no memory<br>
of ever seeing you.<br>
<br>
Stirring stories I heard<br>
about your distant deeds,<br>
and I felt a link with you<br>
though never saw your face.<br>
<br>
I asked you in my heart,<br>
"How long, how far from here<br>
has questing taken you?<br>
Does destiny intend for me<br>
someday to hear your voice?"<br>
<br>
My white-haired years<br>
now tell me it is I<br>
who traveled out upon<br>
that long, far journey.<br>
<br>
Soon I will be coming back<br>
to share my life's adventures<br>
with you in a place not<br>
far away nor danger-filled,<br>
a place as near as breath and pulse.<br>
<br>
I've missed your easy laugh<br>
and kindly voice, dear friend,<br>
but soon enough we'll meet again<br>
to pray the prayers of ancient days.<br>
</font>
</td></tr></tbody></table>

<a name="Ones"><h4>Ones</h4></a> 
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
I spot a one.<br>
He changes lanes abruptly<br>
right in front of me, no signal.<br>
My teeth clench.<br>
He is number one in his machismo,<br>
and I a separate one in irritation.<br>
<br>
Another one is following my car<br>
close enough to fill my mirror.<br>
I want to slow down<br>
and teach him a lesson,<br>
but instead I simmer along<br>
as one trapped.<br>
<br>
I notice my cozy tailgater is flying<br>
an American flag above his window,<br>
loyal in some kind of patriotism,<br>
separate in some kind of jingoism,<br>
and I explore my intolerance.<br>
<br>
By "ones" I mean sequestered minds,<br>
"me" people in a universe of "not me."<br>
Ones will celebrate their personal glory<br>
then perish into their self-created void.<br>
Ones will say we go around just once,<br>
done, with no later come-arounds,<br>
so that when the gustoed body quits,<br>
the mind joins Big Zero forever.<br>
<br>
Why don't I think the same as that?<br>
With not one proof that holds a drop,<br>
I see a future human state<br>
unhindered by me-centric rivalries.<br>
<br>
Birthing time and time again,<br>
evolving life by life eternally,<br>
it seems to me we'll someday<br>
give up being ones, and enter<br>
fully the community of Unity<br>
where competition isn't.<br>
<br>
Though now I seem a one<br>
to any other one<br>
as the other one, for now,<br>
may seem a one to me,<br>
I hear an inner-speaking<br>
Spirit say that all of us<br>
are one with Utmost One<br>
and separated mainly by<br>
our walled-off minds and<br>
pretty bags of bones.<br>
</font>
</td></tr></tbody></table>

<a name="Remembrance"><h4>Remembrance</h4></a> 
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">

Remembering tells me<br>
I was never not, nor<br>
were you nor anyone.<br>
<br>
Arteries in the Cosmos<br>
are pulsing with light<br>
and life and love<br>
<br>
in a flow never ceasing<br>
yet constantly changing<br>
in form and expression.<br>
<br>
Peace it is to remember<br>
these arteries that feed<br>
from out of the Unseen,<br>
<br>
their pulsings uncountable,<br>
their inner motions subtler<br>
than any evening breeze.<br>
<br>
Remembering upward<br>
and inward, how not feel<br>
vitality from the One?<br>
<br>
I remember (don't you?)<br>
the beauty within trust,<br>
the safety of community,<br>
<br>
the triumph of cooperation,<br>
the brave sureness of joy,<br>
love as easy to find as air.<br>
<br>
Remembering as I do<br>
and perhaps as you do,<br>
how could one not return?<br>
</font>
</td></tr></tbody></table>

<a name="BuginMyKitchen"><h4>Bug in My Kitchen</h4></a> 
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
Let me guess,<br>
box-elder bug<br>
on my kitchen floor,<br>
that you know neither<br>
how you came<br>
to be lost in here<br>
nor how you will<br>
get out--but you will.<br>
<br>
Fright-propelled boat,<br>
six-oared, you worry<br>
the woodwork then<br>
hasten across<br>
the open gloss<br>
and disappear<br>
beneath my stove.<br>
<br>
I shall not hunt you<br>
nor shall we ever<br>
meet again.<br>
<br>
I am just as adrift<br>
on this waxed world<br>
as you were on my floor,<br>
and yet I feel certain<br>
I will someday find<br>
a serendipitous stove<br>
to mask my out-passing.<br>
</font>
</td></tr></tbody></table>

<a name="UnclosedLoops"><h4>Unclosed Loops</h4></a> 
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
Life after rollicking life<br>
I have littered<br>
and frittered<br>
but mostly learned<br>
within unclosed loops.<br>
<br>
The room where I work<br>
is a monument to<br>
get-out-and-leave-out<br>
and all my other rooms<br>
imitate such open loops.<br>
<br>
Shall I dare to suggest<br>
that every spiral<br>
is an unclosed loop?<br>
And point out that spirals<br>
are the basis of life<br>
on all of its planes?<br>
<br>
Closed-loop people<br>
I have seen, dazzling<br>
in their neatness,<br>
smilingly prompt,<br>
dickensly proud<br>
of their punctilious<br>
buttoned-downishness.<br>
<br>
Do devotees of closed loops<br>
expire with a snap, I wonder?<br>
And will I expire someday<br>
with an ambiguous sigh?<br>
<br>
Let's broadly hint that<br>
perhaps people never do expire<br>
but instead subscribe over time<br>
to suitably-spiraled-up bodies,<br>
incremental costumes for playing<br>
parts in this human drama<br>
of infinite run.  "Death" is all<br>
the rage these eons, but only<br>
for those who think their eyes<br>
see all there is to see.<br>
<br>
Let's even risk wondering<br>
whether supposedly closed loops<br>
might be minor quanta within major<br>
evolving spirals.<br>
<br>
Unclosed as my loops are,<br>
I admit to irritating the tidy.<br>
Closed, the tidy may enjoy<br>
their control, but beyond<br>
their cubishness a universe<br>
swirls with intranesting<br>
spirals that may little praise<br>
the painful righteousness<br>
of an organized desk drawer.<br>
<br>
Now, where is that CD<br>
I bought yesterday?<br>
Has it spiraled off?<br>
</font>
</td></tr></tbody></table>
