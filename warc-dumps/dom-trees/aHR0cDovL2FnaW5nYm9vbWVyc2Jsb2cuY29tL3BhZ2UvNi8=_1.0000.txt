				<h2><a href="http://agingboomersblog.com/child-please/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link: Child Please">Child Please</a></h2>
				<div class="post-content">
				<p><span style="color: #000000;"><strong><span style="color: #ff6600;"><em>Child please</em>, the other night I put my feet up,</span></strong> <span style="color: #000000;">turned on the boob tube, flipped over to the On Demand selections, and ended up vegging out for close to five hours mesmerized by HBO Sports’ highly watchable, <strong><em>Hard Knocks</em></strong>.</span></span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Now, other than for professional boxing, I’m not a big sports or football fan, but for some inexplicable reason, I enjoy reality shows and/ or documentaries about athletes, especially the slickly-produced HBO offerings.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">With that bit of background, the point of this morning’s entry is to help push a new phrase right smack, dead-on into the lexicon of American English.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Drum roll, please…</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">And the new phrase — the fabulously wonderful new phrase — is…<br>
</span></p>
<blockquote><p><span style="color: #000000;">Child please.</span></p></blockquote>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Now, I know this two word combination seems simple enough on the surface, but in reality it’s a deliciously expressive and wonderful new way to say <em>Fuck you</em> when you’re in life or social situations where saying the real thing would get your ass kicked halfway into next Thursday.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">For example, your spouse starts nagging about the lawn needing mowing.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Instead of making up some lame excuse <strong>if you’re in a good mood</strong> (<em>Dear, I tripped this morning getting the newspaper and injured my Achilles tendon so that landscaping work will just have to wait until next weekend…</em>) or — <strong>if you’re feeling aggressive </strong> — escalating the situation with a <em>Get off your fat bum and do it yourself</em>, you’d simply say…</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;"><em>Child, please.</em></span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Using those two words, especially if pronounced in a slow and loving tone of voice will befuddle your better half (if he/she hasn’t yet read this blog entry or watched <strong><em>Hard Knocks</em></strong>) and the chances are good the lawn will stay unmowed, and you’ll win this particular marital tiff!</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">We have American football wide receiver for the Cincinnati Bengals of the NFL</span><span style="color: #000000;"> </span><span style="color: #000000;"> </span><a title="Learn more about Chad Ochocinco" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chad_Ochocinco">Chad Javon Ochocinco</a><span style="color: #000000;"> (born Chad Javon Johnson on January 9, 1978, in Miami, Florida) </span><span style="color: #000000;"><span style="color: #000000;">to thank for bringing <em>Child please</em> into the English language, and Ocho (dare I stoop so familiar?) does so with a depth of thought and understanding of language and nuance that I’d never given him credi</span>t for.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">You don’t believe me?</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Watch the video where Chad explains just a few of the more subtle aspects of what I predict will join Sarah Palin’s <em>Death Panels</em> </span><span style="color: #000000;"> (and, yes, due to popular demand from loyal readers I shall write one day soon about the <em><strong>Alaskan Word Mangler</strong></em>)</span><span style="color: #000000;"> as one of America’s most creative expressions:</span></p>
<div style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #000000;"><object classid="clsid:d27cdb6e-ae6d-11cf-96b8-444553540000" width="420" height="339" codebase="http://download.macromedia.com/pub/shockwave/cabs/flash/swflash.cab#version=6,0,40,0"><param name="allowFullScreen" value="true"><param name="allowScriptAccess" value="always"><param name="src" value="http://www.dailymotion.com/swf/xa70pf_hard-knocks-s05e01-chad-ochocinco-c_fun"><param name="allowfullscreen" value="true"><embed type="application/x-shockwave-flash" width="420" height="339" src="http://www.dailymotion.com/swf/xa70pf_hard-knocks-s05e01-chad-ochocinco-c_fun" allowscriptaccess="always" allowfullscreen="true"></object><em><a href="http://www.dailymotion.com/thegame91"></a></em></span></div>
<p style="text-align: left;"><span style="color: #000000;"><br>
</span></p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><span style="color: #000000;">Now, seriously, I bet you’ve&nbsp; joined me in no longer thinking of  Chad Ochocinco as just another self-aggrandizing loud mouth. In fact, you’re no doubt now including Ochocinco with a peer group of brilliant word and phrase creators like…</span></p>
<ul>
<li><span style="color: #000000;">England’s <a title="Learn more about that brilliant rascal Dr. Johnson" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dr._Samuel_Johnson">Dr. Samuel Johnson</a> (September 18, 1709 – December 13, 1784)</span></li>
<li><span style="color: #000000;">French lexicographer <a title="Learn more about Pierre Larousse" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre_Larousse">Pierre Larousse</a> (October 23, 1817-January 3, 1875)</span></li>
<li><span style="color: #000000;">America’s noted <a title="He looked goofy, but Noah Webster was smart, as you'll see if you click here." href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noah_Webster">Noah Webster</a> (October 16, 1758 – May 28, 1843)</span></li>
<li><span style="color: #000000;">Russian lexicographer <a title="Soon, all aging male boomers will look like this if Obama's Death Panels pass into law!" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vladimir_Dal">Vladimir Dal</a> (November 10, 1801 – September 22, 1872)</span></li>
<li><span style="color: #000000;">and, of course, American linguist, philosopher, cognitive scientist, political activist, author, and lecturer <a title="Learn more about Noam Chomsky" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noam_Chomsky">Avram Noam Chomsky</a></span> <span style="color: #000000;">(pronounced /?no?m ?t??mski/; born December 7, 1928).</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Well, even though I feel confident I’ll be revisiting the <strong>Child Please </strong>topic in the future (and no doubt even using the phrase on occasion in these humble scribblings), I think this would be a good place to stop for now.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Oh, one more thing… </span></p>
<p style="padding-left: 30px;"><span style="color: #000000;">… if you’re offended by some of the words or phrases used in this blog entry, I have only one thing to say to any rude comments you might leave below…</span></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #000000;"><strong>Child, please!</strong></span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Seriously, good God in Heaven (if there is such a thing — that’s a topic for another day), what a wonderful new addition to the vocabulary of contemporary America.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;"><span style="color: #000000;">Thank you my new wordsmith hero, Chad Ochocinco!</span><br>
</span></p>

				<h2><a href="http://agingboomersblog.com/baby-boomer-drugs/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link: Baby Boomer Drugs">Baby Boomer Drugs</a></h2>
				<div class="post-content">
				<p><span style="color: #000000;"><span style="color: #ff6600;"><strong>While goofing off today when I should</strong></span> <span style="color: #000000;">have been working, I had to smile at the following story, which says at least some <span style="text-decoration: underline;">bad old baby boomers</span></span> are still getting high:<br>
</span></p>
<h3><strong><span style="color: #993300;">Baby Boomers Still Using Illegal Drugs</span></strong></h3>
<p style="padding-left: 30px;">Some baby boomers are continuing to use illicit drugs as they grow older. The number of 50-somethings who say they took drugs within the past year has nearly doubled from 5.1 percent in 2002 to 9.4 percent in 2007, according to a new analysis by the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA).</p>
<p style="padding-left: 30px;">Among all other age groups the rates of drug use stayed the same or decreased. Over the same time period marijuana use among Americans age 50 to 59 increased from 3.1 to 5.7 percent and nonmedical utilization of prescription drugs climbed to 4 percent in 2007 from 2.2 percent in 2002.</p>
<p style="text-align: right;"><a title="Bad old boomers still drugging!" href="http://www.usnews.com/blogs/planning-to-retire/2009/08/20/baby-boomers-still-using-illegal-drugs.html">Click here to read whole story…</a></p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><span style="color: #000000;">Well now, I don’t know about you, but I’d much rather see my fellow boomers having a toke now and again than I would see them scarfing down the horrible so-called “medications” that the “your doctor” types prescribe so willy nilly.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><span style="color: #000000;">I mean, seriously, it’s stunning to me how stupid most people must be who watch prescription drug commercials on TV and who then go to their doctors and actually request the meds they’ve seen promoted so vigorously on the boob tube.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><span style="color: #000000;">Really, what kind of idiot are you if you think TV advertised drugs are safe when almost 30 seconds of many of the one minute commercials are devoted to monstrous side effects like…</span></p>
<ul>
<li><span style="color: #000000;">suicidal thoughts</span></li>
<li><span style="color: #000000;">insomnia</span></li>
<li><span style="color: #000000;">incontinence</span></li>
<li><span style="color: #000000;">nausea</span></li>
<li><span style="color: #000000;">depression</span></li>
<li><span style="color: #000000;">an erection lasting four hours</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Well, maybe the last one might not be so monstrous… though at four hours, whoa, it just could be… really monstrous. </span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">But I digress. </span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Back to the subject at hand.<br>
</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">I guess the point of this little rant is that the endless drug commercials on TV tend to piss me off, especially when <strong>so many fellow Americans are locked in prisons for using drugs that are less potent and significantly less dangerous</strong> than the junk the medical community and pharmaceutical goons are dealing to gullible senior citizens (as well as younger people) in this country.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">What’s the difference between legal and illegal drugs in this country?</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Not much. </span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">You get both from pushers of one stripe or another, don’t you?</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">How absurd is it that some of the pushers spend much of their lives in prison for selling their junk while some of the other pushers vacation in the Bahamas for selling their “medications”?</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Something stinks up the joint in this equation, don’t you think?</span></p>
<p><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-14" title="sigfont" src="http://agingboomersblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/02/sigfont.gif" alt="sigfont" width="88" height="31"></p>

				<h2><a href="http://agingboomersblog.com/how-to-be-happy/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link: How to Be Happy">How to Be Happy</a></h2>
				<div class="post-content">
				<p><span style="color: #ff6600;"><strong>For today’s meditation, let’s turn to</strong></span> <span style="color: #000000;">American novelist and short story writer, Willa Cather, who tells us how to be happy…</span></p>
<blockquote><p><span style="color: #000000;">That is happiness: to be dissolved into something complete and great.</span></p></blockquote>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">You are already complete and great. Right now. In this very moment.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">But you most likely don’t realize your completeness and greatness because <strong>your constant mind chatter and thinking</strong> about the past and the future are blocking the real you.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">So bust through the block.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Close your eyes.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Take a deep breath. Hold for a few seconds. Exhale slowly. Repeat until you’ve slowed down, decreased your thinking, and deepened your awareness.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;"><strong>There it is.</strong></span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">You.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Total, complete, and great.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Amazing.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">And now you know how to be happy anytime you want to be. <img src="http://agingboomersblog.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif" alt=":)" class="wp-smiley"><br>
</span></p>

				<h2><a href="http://agingboomersblog.com/poetry-quote/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link: Poetry Quote">Poetry Quote</a></h2>
				<div class="post-content">
				<p><span style="color: #000000;"><span style="color: #ff6600;"><strong>I’ve been swamped with putting on</strong></span> the final touches of my <a title="Chet's detox diet and natural health program" href="http://chetday.com/21day.html">21 Days to Health &amp; Beyond</a> detox diet and rejuvenation program the past week, and I’m still buried today in last minute details for tomorrow’s launch.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">But I do want to share a quick quote here on the blog to keep the cobwebs from gathering too thickly while I’m working.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">This quote comes from one of my favorite poets, Theodore Roethke, who wrote…</span></p>
<blockquote><p><span style="color: #000000;">Being, not doing, is my first joy.</span></p></blockquote>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Oh, that’s nice, isn’t it?</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">And so hard to do these days when most of us aging boomers (and everyone else for that matter) are running around like chickens with their heads cut off trying to salvage what we once thought might be some retirement days of rest and relaxation after a life time of hard, brutal, soul-deadening, <em>grind it til you drop</em> working for a living.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Well, I don’t know about you, but later today I’m going to take some time and just be.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">If you have a favorite quote from a poet, there’s plenty of space here on the blog to comment and share it, eh? <img src="http://agingboomersblog.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif" alt=":)" class="wp-smiley"> </span></p>

				<h2><a href="http://agingboomersblog.com/to-kill-a-mockingbird/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link: To Kill a Mockingbird">To Kill a Mockingbird</a></h2>
				<div class="post-content">
				<p><span style="color: #000000;"><span style="color: #ff6600;"><strong>First, today’s commentary was inspired</strong></span> by a <a title="Interesting thread on favorite books" href="http://www.thegeminiweb.com/babyboomer/?p=2610">topic</a> devoted to listing one’s favorite books at <a title="The Boomer Chronicles home page" href="http://www.thegeminiweb.com/babyboomer/">The Boomer Chronicles</a>, one of my favorite blogs for aging boomers.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">It’s interesting that contemporary opinion varies as widely as it does regarding the literary worth of&nbsp; <em>To Kill a Mockingbird</em>, <span style="color: #000000;">but, then, I think that’s been true for decades now.</span></span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">As a novelist and former high school English teacher, I think <em>Mockingbird</em> ends up on the “best books” list of so many people because most kids encounter it in school at some point.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">And, compared to other required readings on middle and high school English curriculum lists, <em>Mockingbird </em>is a thrilling and highly readable deviation from the <strong>usual boring, hardly relevant</strong> to today’s kids, and difficult to understand “classic.”</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">(That’s if any classics are still required reading in public schools in America; they were certainly on their way out the last time I taught English back in 1996.)</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Skill of writing (Lee’s no Shakespeare) and thematic depth of message (nor is she an Umberto Eco) aside, <em>Mockingbird </em>is <strong>a powerful and important read for young people</strong> because the characters are so vividly drawn and easy to identify with.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Additionally, the compelling and dramatic plot draws the reader along page after page after page.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">I had many students during my teaching daze tell me, “Mr. Day, geez, thanks for finally picking a good book that’s fun to read. I finished it last night.”</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;"><strong>This sort of comment</strong> came from both the students who were always several days ahead of the required pages to read as well as the students whose reading abilities made a snail’s pace look speedy.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">I agree that, as “literature,” <em>To Kill a Mockingbird</em> leaves much to be desired when compared to writers like Shakespeare (<span style="text-decoration: underline;">who sets the bar for everyone else as far as I’m concerned</span>), Faulkner, Joyce, et al.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">But I also believe <em>To Kill a Mockingbird</em> has a firm place on the “best books” list because most people will remember their happy reading experience with it long after they’re forgotten the painful memories associated with being forced to plow through “classics” they found irrelevant to their lives and inaccessible to their minds.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">In closing, if you have a fond (or not so fond) memory about reading <em>To Kill a Mockingbird</em>, I hope you’ll comment and share it below.</span></p>

				<h2><a href="http://agingboomersblog.com/bill-gates-quits-facebook/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link: Bill Gates Quits Facebook">Bill Gates Quits Facebook</a></h2>
				<div class="post-content">
				<p><span style="color: #000000;"><span style="color: #ff6600;"><strong>I was pleasantly surprised this morning</strong></span> when I logged into <a title="Link to Google News" href="http://www.google.com/news">Google News</a> and learned that the world’s wealthiest man, Bill Gates, had decided to give up on <a title="Bill's Facebook page" href="http://www.facebook.com/s.php?q=bill+gates&amp;init=quick#/pages/Bill-Gates/42482194446?ref=search">his Facebook account</a> because “it was just too much trouble.”</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Well, for once, I applaud something Gates has done because I agree with him that trying to keep up with social networking on sites like <em>Facebook </em>and <em>MySpace </em>is, to be frank and honest, a big pain in the ass.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">I mean, seriously, who has the time to even answer the questions asked by the sites themselves during registration, much less to then get involved with all the silliness that comes afterwards when friends, relatives, and strangers start flocking to your page <strong>begging to become your friend</strong> so they can then endlessly share the trivia and detritus of their often inane, boring lives with you?<br>
</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">If people want to have friends, why not do it in the real world instead of over some stupid computer? Get together for a beer… go play Putt Putt, take a hike in the mountains… jog together in a nice park… </span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">I mean, seriously, I can think of a zillion better ways to make and have friends than sitting on your ass in a chair <strong>wasting bandwidth sending goofy messages and lame images</strong> (usually out of focus and poorly composed) back and forth until it’s well past the witching hour every night.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">I know that’s a dinosaur outlook in this day and age of online relationships, but, hey, I’m getting aged enough that I’m almost 99% convinced now that the <strong>old ways were (and are) the best ways</strong>.<br>
</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">And if you’re <strong>generally sick of people and mainly want a little peace and quiet </strong>in your life, then dropping all your social networking accounts makes a lot of sense.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">With that said, I occasionally post on <a title="Chet's Twitter page" href="http://twitter.com/chetday">Twitter</a>, which I don’t find particularly useful or interesting, though I haven’t yet closed my account since my son tells me doing so would make me even more of an old fogey than I already am.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">According to the story I read about Gates quitting <em>Facebook</em>:</span></p>
<blockquote><p><span style="color: #000000;">Gates was in the Indian capital to receive the <em>Indira Gandhi Prize for Peace, Disarmament and Development</em>, awarded by the government for his work for the charitable organisation, the <em>Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation</em>.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">The foundation, built by his massive fortune, has committed nearly one billion dollars to health and development projects in India, targeting especially AIDS and polio.</span></p></blockquote>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">I’ll save my rant for another time on why I think the world’s richest man is spending billions to vaccinate hundreds of millions in foreign countries all around the world.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">And that’s my admittedly disjointed and not fully realized rant for today.</span></p>

<blockquote><p><span style="color: #000000;">To be wronged is nothing unless you continue to remember it.</span></p></blockquote>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">This week, focus on letting go of all those instances when you were wronged in life by somebody or something.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;"><span style="color: #000000;">Here’s a simple way to let go…</span></span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Take three or four long, slow, deep breaths, focus on the pauses between breaths, and feel your consciousness shift into that calm well of silence that is the real you.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">When you’re calm and centered, think about the most recent instance of when you believe someone did you wrong.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Feel the anger, resentment, or other negative emotion associated with that perceived wrong.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Now, breathe deeply and, as you exhale, let go completely of all that emotional weight that has been holding you down.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Ah, relief.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Repeat this exercise as often as you wish. If you work diligently with this technique, you can let go of every wronged instance in your whole life.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">And if you let go of all that old baggage, you’ll feel as light as a feather on a soft breeze.</span></p>

				<div class="post-content">
				<p><span style="color: #000000;"><span style="color: #ff6600;"><strong>Damn, I mean seriously, I was outside</strong></span> last evening minding my own business while mowing the lawn when, kaboom, out of the blue I get stung by a flying insect, most likely a wasp (they tend to hand out in the sloped and untended area of my lawn.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Wasp bite right smack on the third phalange of my right ring finger.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">So I stop the mower and go into the house and ice the sting down. Not so bad, I think.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Back outside, I resume mowing and, pow, a second wasp plants another string on me, right on the shinbone of my right leg.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">This one hurts like the dickens. </span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Immediate, intense stab of pain like a <strong>dentist angrily pushing his drill deep into a molar root </strong>that hadn’t been numbed.<br>
</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">I mean I’m cussing up a storm and yelling like a manic, willing at the moment to spout off — if I only knew them — any magic words that would disappear every wasp, hornet, bee, or other flying insect from my little piece of this wonderous world.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">So I stop the mower again and for a second time run into the house to ice down this latest assault on my poor old aging boomer’s body.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Well, I finish the mowing job, leaving undone the area that obviously has a nest of some sort in the vicinity, and then go in and shower and sit around while watching the bites swell up and redden.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">All this wouldn’t be quite so annoying if I hadn’t had <strong>the same thing happen two weeks ago</strong> when I mowed the lawn. And that sting from a bee (this one left the stinger in me so I knew it was a bee) was no darn fun for almost a week before the swelling went down and the pain disappeared.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">So now it’s Saturday and I’m sitting here bitching about the two latest stings because they hurt like a visit to a sadistic dentist whose instruments include pliers and bailing wire and have me all distracted from doing the writing I had planned on doing today.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">The point of all this, which I apologize for getting to so late, is that <strong>today I have an increased appreciation for anyone who lives in chronic pain</strong>.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Seriously, I don’t know how folks in continual pain do it when the small but constant and annoying pains from my two stings have reduced my overall capacity today to do anything but yell at the dogs and my wife by at least 30%.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Cranky? </span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Man, I’m so cranky I could yell nasty things at the twelve disciples of Christ… I could laugh right now at the Pope’s and Hillary’s broken wrists… <strong>I could pee on Michelle Obama’s&nbsp; organic garden</strong>… it’s bad… it’s really bad when my usually good-natured, good-humored self scales this height of crabbiness!</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Well, enough of all this. </span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Time to stop whining and go ice down the wasp bites again for a few minutes. </span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Ice so far is the only thing that helps much.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">I was up four times between midnight and eight a.m. icing down these stupid wasp bites because every time I’d roll over little jolts of increased pain would wake me up.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Poor, poor, poor me!</span><span style="color: #000000;"> <img src="http://agingboomersblog.com/wp-includes/images/smilies/icon_smile.gif" alt=":)" class="wp-smiley"> </span></p>
