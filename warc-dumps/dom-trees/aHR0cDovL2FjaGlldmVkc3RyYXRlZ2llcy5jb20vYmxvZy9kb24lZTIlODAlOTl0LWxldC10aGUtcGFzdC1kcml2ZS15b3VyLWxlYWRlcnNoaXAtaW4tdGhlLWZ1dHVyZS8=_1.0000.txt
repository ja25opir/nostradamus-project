						<a title="Don’t Let the Past Drive Your Leadership in the Future" href="http://achievedstrategies.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2010/09/Road_AheadMedium.jpg" rel="lightbox"><img src="http://achievedstrategies.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2010/09/Road_AheadMedium.jpg" alt="Don’t Let the Past Drive Your Leadership in the Future" width="180" height="120" class="thumbnail alignright"></a>						<h2><a href="http://achievedstrategies.com/blog/don%e2%80%99t-let-the-past-drive-your-leadership-in-the-future/" rel="bookmark" title="Don’t Let the Past Drive Your Leadership in the Future">Don’t Let the Past Drive Your Leadership in the Future</a></h2>
						<p class="post-details">Posted on 15. Sep, 2010 by  <a href="http://achievedstrategies.com/blog/author/admin/" title="Posts by Shawn Murphy">Shawn Murphy</a> in <a href="http://achievedstrategies.com/blog/category/change-tactics/" title="View all posts in Change In Action" rel="category tag">Change In Action</a>, <a href="http://achievedstrategies.com/blog/category/leadership/" title="View all posts in Leadership" rel="category tag">Leadership</a>, <a href="http://achievedstrategies.com/blog/category/people-change/" title="View all posts in People &amp; Change" rel="category tag">People &amp; Change</a></p>
						
						                        
						<p><span style="color: #000000;">I want to share a personal story to make an important point for leaders in business. First, the personal story.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">For overly 30 years my dad and I were not on speaking terms.&nbsp; I had a belief that he wasn’t a good person.&nbsp; I based that belief on my perception that he was rarely around when I was growing up.&nbsp; I carried the burden of anger and resentment of 30+ years.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Through some personal work I uncovered a sense of loss over the lost relationship with my dad.&nbsp; I picked up the phone and we rebooted our relationship. I told my dad that it was no longer acceptable that he wasn’t a part of my life.&nbsp; I missed him at the holidays and wanted to experience father/son time with him.&nbsp; We get to do that today.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">I could get really sappy on you, but I’ll save that and transition into the business point I want to make.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">I allowed distant, fuzzy memories to dictate what I could have today and in the future – a relationship with my dad.&nbsp; I believed my dad was no good then and he could be no good now.&nbsp; Right? How often does this pattern of logic happen in business? By leaders? Too often.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Too often we make filing errors when it comes to our past.&nbsp; Here’s an example of this error: As a leader you make the mistake of accepting help from a peer on a project. Your peer drops the ball and leaves you hanging.&nbsp; You end up having to complete the work your peer didn’t do.&nbsp; So you say to yourself, “I’ll never do that again.&nbsp; If I want something done, I’ll have to do it myself.” &nbsp;There’s the filing error.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Not everyone will leave you hanging. Letting the situation (now in the past) determine who you allow to help you in the future eliminates your ability to be a contributing team member.&nbsp; As a leader, a top priority is to create an environment that allows others to do their best work.&nbsp; And best work often requires team input.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">What’s an alternative way to respond to the situation? Find out what happened with your coworker and let her know her behavior is unacceptable.&nbsp; Let the problem serve as a lesson to you and move forward.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">I operate on this simply tenant in similar situations: When there’s a “mess,” clean it up and move on.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">I learned with my dad that I’d miss out on great moments and interactions if the baggage of the past tugs on my mind.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">As leaders, we are severely crippled in causing great moments and having meaningful interactions when we let the past drive our leadership today and in the future.</span>
