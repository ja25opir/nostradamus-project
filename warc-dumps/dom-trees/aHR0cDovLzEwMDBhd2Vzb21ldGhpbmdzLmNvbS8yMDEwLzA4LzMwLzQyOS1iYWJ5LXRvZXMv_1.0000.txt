						<h2>#429 Baby&nbsp;toes</h2>
				
						<div class="entry">
															<span class="jump"><a href="http://1000awesomethings.com/2010/08/30/429-baby-toes/#comments">Jump to Comments</a></span>
								<br class="clear">
														<div class="snap_preview"><p><a href="http://1000awesomethings.files.wordpress.com/2010/08/baby-toes.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-11143" style="border:0 none;margin:10px;" title="baby toes" src="http://1000awesomethings.files.wordpress.com/2010/08/baby-toes.jpg?w=237&amp;h=216" alt="" width="237" height="216"></a>We took a trip last weekend.</p>
<p>My friends Mike and Jes had a baby so we jumped in a car and headed down the highway to visit the three-month old pile of diapers, flab, and <strong>giggly charm</strong>.</p>
<p>She was a stunner, too — cute and cuddly, <strong>silent and smiling</strong>, she was passed around like a <strong>hot potato </strong>for a few hours on Saturday afternoon. Now, I have no idea how to hold a baby so when someone passed me the potato I held her up by the armpits about two feet above my head. She gave me a patient smile and a weary sigh that seemed to say <strong>“Buddy, seriously?”</strong></p>
<p><a href="http://1000awesomethings.files.wordpress.com/2010/08/hot-potato.jpg"><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-11146" style="border:0 none;margin:10px;" title="hot potato" src="http://1000awesomethings.files.wordpress.com/2010/08/hot-potato.jpg?w=169&amp;h=169" alt="" width="169" height="169"></a>While I had her up there I couldn’t stop staring at her tiny feet. I mean, they were the size of <strong>hotel shampoo bottles </strong>and had ten wiggling toes searching for support way up in the living room stratosphere. All the toes were so small and had microscopic little toenails — even that tiny baby toenail, which as we all know never changes in size.</p>
<p>It’s fun picturing how much those <strong>tiny feet </strong>are going to do in the future. They’ll be walking this baby every single day, every step of the way. Wrapped in fresh runners on the first day of school, tied tightly in <strong>ballet slippers </strong>for the big recital, jammed into cleats for the weekend ball tournament, and wedged in hiking shoes for trips cross country. From trampolines to treadmills, <strong>from backyards to backpacking</strong>, and everything in between, these tiny feet will be clocking up and chalking up everywhere she’s going … and everywhere she’s been.</p>
<p>AWESOME!</p>
<p><a href="http://1000awesomethings.files.wordpress.com/2010/08/running-away-in-bare-feet.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-11158" title="running away in bare feet" src="http://1000awesomethings.files.wordpress.com/2010/08/running-away-in-bare-feet-e1283119102471.jpg?w=235&amp;h=294" alt="" width="235" height="294"></a></p>
