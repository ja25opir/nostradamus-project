						<h2>A DUTCH&nbsp;CRUISE</h2>
				
						<div class="entry">
															<span class="jump"><a href="http://arsenal-opinion.com/2009/10/21/a-dutch-cruise/#comments">Jump to Comments</a></span>
								<br class="clear">
														<script type="text/javascript" src="http://s0.wp.com/wp-content/plugins/adverts/adsense.js?m=1263084110g&amp;1"></script><p>It was apparent from minutes into the game with AZ that Le Boss had told his stars to keep the tempo down, to take it easy and to avoid any rash decisions or challenges that could lead to injury.</p>
<p>The premiership title has been blown wide open and you can truly sense a shift in power in England at the moment. Paul Merson said before kick-off that in his opinion, ‘Arsenal look like the best team in the country and are not a mile behind Barcelona!’.</p>
<p>While some of my amoeba minded friends sat and scoffed at such a comment and touted Chelsea and United as the same-old same-old favourites, I sat pondering our former player’s words.</p>
<p>Yes, both we and Chelsea have two losses under our belts. However, our first (Manchester United) was a dubious one at that having bossed the game for 90% of its duration and then a rather unscrupulous penalty from Wayne ‘Tom Daley’ Rooney followed by an own goal led to our grossly unfair defeat.</p>
<p>The second yet another trip to Manchester which I’m not going to delve into, suffice to say I will be getting my ticket in April for their return to the Emirates although I’m sure the man I’m really going to see will have a cold, flu, poorly leg, smallpox etc</p>
<p>Chelsea came at Wigan (4-0 Arsenal) and then Villa Park, both they deserved to lose.</p>
<p>But Mr. Merson is right, before last night we had won seven games in a row scoring twenty one goals, amazing!</p>
<p>It was incredible to listen to Merson saying our spine of Gallas, Vermaelen, Song, and Fabregas was actually our strong point, having been exposed as painfully lacking by Man United in the semis of this very competition last year at the Emirates.</p>
<p>We also have several players ready to slot into that area, Ramsey, Denilson and dare I say, Abou Diaby?</p>
<p>We then have our little attacking stars (Fabregas of course is not simply a defensive spinal player) such as our skipper, Arshavin of course, Van Persie, Walcott, Nasri, Rosicky, Eduardo etc.</p>
<p>Arsenal cruised last night however, with Vermaelen and Song my favourite players clearly showing what style of game Wenger was playing.</p>
<p>While one nil would have been superb, one-one away in Holland to the Dutch Champions courtesy of a 93<sup>rd</sup> minute goal is hardly the end of all things!</p>
<p>It could be a lot worse, I mean, you could be beaten at home, couldn’t you Liverpool? Barcelona? (Is it wrong I actually got physical pleasure from seeing Barsenal lose to Rubin at the Nou Camp?’)</p>
<p>Arsenal have easily qualified in this group, so easy in fact was it that I wasn’t even paying attention the Liege score, and in truth still don’t know it now!</p>
<p>It was a solid, if a little dull performance at times tonight and I would have been singing their praises had we not got pegged back last night, but still, in third gear we looked as if we were humouring a very, very good team last night.</p>
<p>As I say there were a couple of stand-out performances Thomas Vermaelen and Alex Song I thought were the two best by far but Arshavin had a good game (hideous back pass from inside opposition territory giving a corner aside!). RVP had a solid game as did Fabregas (not fantastic, but then, we can’t expect what he’s been treating us to every week now, can we?’</p>
<p>My player scores:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Mannone</strong> – 6. To be honest, didn’t have a lot to do, didn’t think he could do a whole lot about the goal. Perhaps he could have come to the ball but perhaps that’s super critical. Nice to have some competition in this area.</li>
<li><strong>Sagna</strong> – 6. Fair, not great. Some good runs, hooked up with the advance players well.</li>
<li><strong>Gallas</strong> – 7. Played well, was solid enough, really looks as if he enjoys playing with the Verminator.</li>
<li><strong>Vermaelen</strong> – 8. Really good game from the CB last night. Absolute bulldog at the back. Put his heads to a ball an AZ player was volleying and won the challenge!</li>
<li><strong>Clichy</strong> – 6. Hasn’t come to life in my opinion this year yet. Really hasn’t clicked, defensively or in an attacking manner, his speed means he can make up for his mistakes that are creeping in far too often.</li>
<li><span style="color:#000000;"><strong>Song – </strong>8. Solid again, cleared up and started off moves, really brilliant.</span></li>
<li><strong>Diaby</strong> – 6. Meh. Some good stuff, some good runs but agree with the commentator, ‘I haven’t really heard Diaby’s name.’ Almost scored an own goal as well, but not really his bad, was it RVP?</li>
<li><strong>Eboue </strong>– 7. Good energy, good willpower, tried and tried and tried. Not a starter in my mind but a good squad player.</li>
<li><strong>Fabregas</strong> -7. Good, some sloppiness but I do really believe Wenger told his boys to take it easy tonight ahead of a weekend trip to West Ham.</li>
<li><strong>Arshavin </strong>– 7. Some moments of brilliance, and one bizarre one! Always looks confident and relished playing as a CF for the last minutes getting two or three shots on target in moments up top. Hint, hint Mr. Wenger.</li>
<li><strong>Van Persie</strong> – 7. Good, good. Some really lovely touches and really should have scored in the first half.</li>
</ul>
<p>As the scores show, the team were on cruise control last night yet still never looked in any trouble or discomfort. Let’s not jinx it guys, but its looking very, very good at the moment.</p>
<p>Frightened Mr. Ferguson, Mr. Ancelotti? Not nearly frightened enough, you have no idea what’s ‘gunning’ for you!</p>
