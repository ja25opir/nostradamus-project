<p><img src="http://aboutmyrecovery.com/wp-content/uploads/2007/04/meattable.jpg" alt="blogger" align="left">I’m preparing for the New Year celebration tonight. While preparing for our noche buena, Butch and I reflected on this year and the <a href="http://aboutmyrecovery.com/2007/12/30/review-of-2007-goals/">2007 goals</a> we achieved or did not achieve.  I’ve learned to be grateful for the positive and negative experiences. They made me a better blogger and person. For everything that happened to me as a blogger this year, there is a reason for which I am grateful.  I will list them all but will not mention the specifics of the negative experience.</p>
<p>1. <b>I received three awards  for my blog this year.</b></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="http://aboutmyrecovery.com/2007/04/01/family-blog-award-and-the-philippine-blog-awards-night/">Globelines Broadband Family Blog Award</a> (in honor of family-oriented blogging) 2007 Philippine Blog Award, March 31, 2007</li>
<li> <a href="http://digitalfilipinowebawards.blogspot.com/2007/04/winners-digitalfilipinocom-web-awards.html">Winner, Blog- Personal Category</a>, <a href="http://digitalfilipinowebawards.blogspot.com/">DigitalFilipino.com Web Awards 2007</a> April 27, 2007</li>
<li><a href="http://aboutmyrecovery.com/2007/11/23/best-website-blogs-category-in-10th-philippine-web-awards-is…/">Winner, Best Website</a>, Blogs Category of the 10th Philippine Web Awards held November 23, 2007</li>
</ul>
<p>The truth is I believe other blogs are more deserving than mine.   Despite that nagging thought, I accept the judges’ perception  and decision with heartfelt gratitude.</p>
<p><span id="more-1487"></span></p>
<p>2. <b>TV appearance</b> in <a href="http://aboutmyrecovery.com/2007/08/25/youtube-video-of-mom-bloggers-at-momworks-lifestyle-show/">Mom Works</a> together with <a href="http://nancydrewandme.blogspot.com">Cathy</a> and <a href="http://ode2old.blogspot.com">Annamanila</a> to show other mothers the benefits of blogging</p>
<p>3. <b>Featured in</b>  Yehey’s <a href="http://www.yehey.com/gadgets/articles.aspx?pn=3&amp;id=167289">Her Life as A Blogger</a>, Lifestyle Inquirer’s <a href="http://showbizandstyle.inquirer.net/lifestyle/lifestyle/view_article.php?article_id=66913">Mother Bloggers</a>, Sunday Inquirer Magazine <a href="http://showbizandstyle.inquirer.net/sim/sim/view_article.php?article_id=91588">Catching the Blog</a>.</p>
<p>3. <b>Resource Speaker</b> in <a href="http://aboutmyrecovery.com/2007/01/06/how-to-promote-your-blog/"> Digital Filipino’s Blogging and Social Networking Forum</a>  for personal blogs and iBlog 3 <a href="http://aboutmyrecovery.com/2007/04/15/iblog-3-personal-blogging-success/">Personal Blogging Success</a>.</p>
<p>4. The risk of <b>writing controversial entries</b> can earn you the ire of certain bloggers who have different opinions than yours. Funny thing is the subject of <a href="http://aboutmyrecovery.com/2007/08/09/ethical-blogging/">this entry</a> and <a href="http://aboutmyrecovery.com/2007/09/11/everything-is-political-and-it-all-begins-with-sex">this</a> didn’t raise much of a fuss. The most valuable lesson I learned from these two entries is to <b>IGNORE</b> their ire . Don’t comment back. Also, some  can’t believe you were really sincere in making amends. What to do? <b>IGNORE, ignore, ignore till they tire.</b> End of controversy, right?</p>
<p>I’ve already let go of the past but it’s worth mentioning because it drove me to be a better person. It taught me to avoid negative and bitchy people and be around fun-loving, positive-minded friends.  It also taught most bloggers a lesson that we <b>agree to disagree</b>. Most bloggers are now able to handle arguments in a reasonable manner without bursting an artery.</p>
<p>5. Got into <b>twittering</b> but I had to lock my Twitter.  Twittering is an avenue for ranting or chatting with followers.  It’s not meant for those who are not your followers. So if you rant a lot, lock that twitter because there are stalkers out there who collect your rants and might throw it to your face one day. </p>
<p>6.  As I entered into <b>more <a href="http://aboutmyrecovery.com/2007/07/18/new-media-publishing/">New Media Events</a></b>, I got introduced to another world of publishing. Of course, a few questioned my ethics and “reputation” as a blogger. First things first. I do not make a living by attending <a href="http://aboutmyrecovery.com/category/business-marketing/new-media/">new media events</a>. I happen to have my own webhosting and real estate business other than blogging, you know. It’s hard to believe but new media events pave the way  to generate  new content for my blogs because I am sometimes lazy to go out of the house. Second, I love meeting people.  Really? Oh and I don’t have to prove that by taking photos just to be seen with celebrities or for link-love exchange. To each their own, right?  So keep those malicious thoughts in check and learn to mind your own business.</p>
<p>7. The countless <b>blogger events and parties</b> that brought new friendships. I don’t know if I can name all of them but  the <a href="http://aboutmyrecovery.com/2007/08/24/13-bonding-moments-at-the-taste-asia-bloggers-party/">Taste Asia</a>, <a href="http://aboutmyrecovery.com/2007/01/29/blog-parteeh-07-photos/ ">Blog Parteeh</a>, the <a href="http://aboutmyrecovery.com/2007/04/01/family-blog-award-and-the-philippine-blog-awards-night/">Philippine Blog Awards</a>, <a href="http://aboutmyrecovery.com/2007/12/01/meet-up-with-bloggers-and-the-asus-eee-pc/">Meeting Shai Coggins</a> are just a few of these meet-ups. </p>
<p>I get a kick out of mingling with young bloggers. It means I get to babble endlessly about techie topics which I can never really discuss with my husband or non-blogger friends. <a href="http://aboutmyrecovery.com/2007/08/08/how-to-smile/">Meet-ups</a> and <a href="http://aboutmyrecovery.com/2007/06/24/dinner-and-conversation-with-the-band-of-bloggers/">parties</a> with Dine, Annamanila, <a href="http://herestolife.wordpress.com/" rel="nofollow">Jane</a>, <a href="http://imomonline.com">Chats</a>, <a href="http://scroochchronicles.blogspot.com/">Cookie</a>, <a href="http://www.thesweetlife-rowena.blogspot.com/" rel="nofollow">Rowena</a>,  <a href="http://momsaroundthecorner.blogspot.com/" rel="nofollow">Salen</a>, <a href="http://mysoulfulthoughts.blogspot.com/" rel="nofollow">Rachel</a> , <a href="http://avocado-express.blogspot.com/" rel="nofollow">Lynn</a>,  <a href="http://fengguillermobrum.com/" rel="nofollow">Feng</a>, <a href="http://ladycess.kadyo.com/" rel="nofollow">Cess</a>, <a href="http://teacherjulie.com/" rel="nofollow">Julie</a>,  <a href="http://zahflo.blogspot.com/" rel="nofollow">Lisaflor</a> and other <a href="http://aboutmyrecovery.com/2007/05/27/mommy-lunch-meetup/">Pinoy Moms Network moms</a> also forged wonderful friendships. </p>
<p>8.  <b>Two new advocacies:</b></p>
<ul>
<li>Revived the <b>Filipina SEO Campaign</b> through the <a href="http://filipinaimages.com">FilipinaImages.com</a>together with <a href="http://dine.racoma.com.ph">Dine</a> and my sister, <a href="http://radiantview.com">Lorna</a>. The advocacy was a product of the “<a href="http://aboutmyrecovery.com/2007/06/27/reshaping-the-sexy-filipina-image-filipino-women">Sexy Mom</a>” controversy. FilipinaImages.com is a collaborative effort of all the bloggers who believe that The Filipina of the Future deserves a more empowered, diverse image online.  Jayvee thinks that <a href="http://abuggedlife.com/2007/11/26/philippine-blogosphere-pitch-why-not-aim-for-the-anvil-awards/">The Filipina Campaign</a> should vie as an entry to the 43rd Anvil Awards.
<p>If you haven’t joined, please <a href="http://filipinaimages.com">Sign up now</a>.</p></li>
<li><a href="http://aboutmyrecovery.com/suicide-prevention/">Suicide Prevention</a> as a result of <a href="http://aboutmyrecovery.com/2007/11/08/children-and-adolescent-suicide/">Mariannet Amper’s death</a>. I’ve also contacted <a href="http://aboutmyrecovery.com/2007/11/10/suicide-media-and-the-catholic-church/">Senator Pia Cayetano</a> who promised to make a followup on the <b>Student Suicide Prevention Act of 2005</b> (<a href="http://www.senate.gov.ph/lis/bill_res.aspx?congress=13&amp;q=SBN-1946">Senate Bill No. 1946</a>) filed on March 3, 2005 by Miriam Defensor Santiago.</li>
</ul>
<p>And so that’s how my year went as a blogger. Now that 2007 is drawing to a close, I like to look back on the year that was and try to improve myself  in the coming year. The new year always offers all of us a new slate, a chance to improve and to learn new things.</p>
<p>Our past is neither a mistake or an accident.  We have been there where we needed to be, with the necessary people. We can embrace our past, with its pain, its mistakes, its glory and even its tragedies. It is uniquely ours. It was intended just for us.</p>
<p>Today, I am right where I need to be. My present circumstances are exactly as they need to be—for now.</p>
