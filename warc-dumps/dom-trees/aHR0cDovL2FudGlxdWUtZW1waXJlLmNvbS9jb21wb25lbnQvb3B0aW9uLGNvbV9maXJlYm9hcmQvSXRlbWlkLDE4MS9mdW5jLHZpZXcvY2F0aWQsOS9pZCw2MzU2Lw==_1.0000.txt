								Julius Strabo was still recovering from his captivity in mercenaries hands inside <i>Hyria</i>. Only a couple of days before, roman legions had taken the city and liberated the prisoners in its dungeons. Appointed again as commander of roman garrison in <i>Tarentum</i>, Julius tried to be up-to-date with every question in his obligations when an <i>optio</i> came in citadel <i>praetorium</i> announcing a new sail in the horizon. Julius watched carefully bandages in his arm, remembering last "new sail" and its consequences...<br>
<br>
- <b>Raise the alarm! Garrison to their positions! </b><br>
<br>
<img src="http://img9.imageshack.us/img9/4868/bythesea.jpg"><br>
<br>
Fortunately, that new visitor in <i>Tarentum</i> harbour wasn't a dangerous traveler. It was a <b>Teutonic</b> vessel of the combined fleet, chosen as messenger ship by <b>Wolfgar</b> due to its lightness and manoeuvrability. Once moored, captain of the ship disembarked, giving Julius a small chest... <br>
<br>
--------------------------------------------<br>
<br>
<b>Marcus Aurelius</b> was sitting in his tent. Despite of mercenaries surrender days before, roman army was still concentrated before the walls of <i>Hyria</i>. There was much to do in the province before leaving. <br>
<br>
He had spent all the afternoon reading and reviewing correspondence arrived from the fleet.  Now, some copies of the letters between <b>Lars Porsenna</b> and <b>Shazir</b> were before him. <b>Marcus</b> had read them carefully, delving into every detail. Finally, he stood up and walk to his trunk in the end of the room and, after taking an old wooden box, he sat again behind his desk and started to write... <br>
<br>
<blockquote>QUOTE:<br><br>
To <b>Shazir Heart of the Flame</b>,<br>
<i>King of Corsairs of Cilicia,<br>
Teacher of the Truths of Zoroaster, <br>
Honored adversary of Rome</i>:<br>
<br>
As you said in your last message, <i>Roman Gods</i> were with us in our last battle, but also <i>Ahura Mazda</i> was with you and yours then. <b>Cilician Corsairs</b> proved their warrior courage and their reputation as proverbial seafarers in our last encounter. <br>
<br>
My ancestors teached me enemies must be both hated and respected, and as far as I'm concerned you have proved to be a noble and honored enemy. I'm no soothsayer either and I cannot still see how is this war going to end, but I assure you I'm pleased of having faced an opponent like you. Perhaps in the future, <i>Fortune</i> will change her plans, and we will meet again in other circunstances. <br>
<br>
<b>May the Daevas always smile on you!!</b><br>
<br>
<br>
<b>Marcus Aurelius</b><br>
<i>Senator and Censor of the Roman Republic</i><br>
<br>
<i>PS.- You will fin a small chest with this letter. Object inside comes from a time out of mind. An honourable enemy gave that armlet to me long time ago, and tradition says it must be handed also to a noble adversary. I give it to you; take care of it... </i><br>
</blockquote><br>
<br>
<b>Marcus</b> opened the box and watched carefully the armlet, recalling past times... <br>
<br>
<b>What will future bring for them all? </b><br>
