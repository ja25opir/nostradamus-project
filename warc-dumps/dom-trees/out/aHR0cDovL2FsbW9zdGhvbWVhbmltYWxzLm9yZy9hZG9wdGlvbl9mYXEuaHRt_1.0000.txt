<p class="Default-Paragraph-Font-P"><span class="Normal-C0">1. (Q) Why am I fostering an animal before I adopt?<br></span><span class="Normal-C1">(A) Almost Home offers a unique
    foster program. You as a potential adopter are given the opportunity to foster an
    animal for two weeks,for the sole purpose of compatibility. It truly is a privilege
    -<wbr> and we are one of the very few Michigan rescues to give our applicants this opportunity.<br><br></span><span class="Normal-C0">2.
    (Q) If I âfoster-<wbr>to-<wbr>adoptâ, does it mean that I will automatically be able to adopt

    given priority to adopt providing that you: <br>a) have been forthright in disclosing
    accurate, complete, and honest information on your application,including your veterinarian
    history.<br>b) abide by the foster guidelines â PLEASE READ YOUR FOSTER AGREEMENT.<br>c)
    demonstrate responsibility in caring for the animal.<br><br></span><span class="Normal-C0">3. (Q) May I return my foster
    prior to the end of the specified foster period?<br></span><span class="Normal-C1">(A) When you sign your foster agreement
    you are making a pledge to keep your foster dog or cat for the agreed-<wbr>upon time.
    This is important because your two-<wbr>week foster makes it possible for us to rescue
    another animal in need. The only exception is when specific prior arrangements have
    been made with a volunteer. If you feel the animal is not getting along with your
    dog, cat, or family member, you must be responsible and use a crate, keep him separated,
    or board at an approved kennel at your expense. <br><br></span><span class="Normal-C0">4. (Q) If my fostered animal gets
    sick, how are his medical bills covered?</span></p>
<p class="Default-Paragraph-Font-P"><span class="Normal-C1">(A) &nbsp;Part of being a responsible owner includes medical care, so we expect you to
    take care of all your animalâs needs. In addition, we expect all potential adopters
    to have their fostered pet examined by their personal vet prior to completing the
    final adoption. If you do hot have a vet, we would be happy to recommend one in your
    area. Our entire veterinary budget is directed to our shelter animals in need. &nbsp;For
    obvious reasons we can not reimburse any of your personal vet expenses when you are
    fostering-<wbr>to-<wbr>adopt. &nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="Default-Paragraph-Font-P"><span class="Normal-C1">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="Default-Paragraph-Font-P"><span class="Normal-C1"> </span><span class="Normal-C0">5. (Q) What if my dog or cat gets a common illness like kennel cough or an upper
    respiratory infection?<br></span><span class="Normal-C1">(A) First of all, Donât panic. Even a dog that has received
    a bordetella vaccine (kennel cough) with us may still contract it since the infection
    has an incubation period. Many cases of kennel cough are self limiting and do not
    require treatment. The infection often needs to run its course for approximately
    7-<wbr>10 days. If the animal has a temperature or stops eating, additional medication
    may be required. &nbsp;In some situations we may have medications available to us through
    the rescue. Contact us by e-<wbr>mail at almosthome1965@gmail.com.</span></p>
<p class="Default-Paragraph-Font-P"><span class="Normal-C1">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="Default-Paragraph-Font-P"><span class="Normal-C0">6. (Q) What if I detect fleas on my fostered animal?<br></span><span class="Normal-C1">(A) Contact your personal vet
    or Almost Home at <a href="mailto:almosthome1965@gmail.com">almosthome1965@gmail.com</a> for advice on which products to use.</span></p>
<p class="Default-Paragraph-Font-P"><span class="Normal-C0">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p class="Default-Paragraph-Font-P"><span class="Normal-C0">7. (Q) How much are the adoption donations and where does my money go?<br></span><span class="Normal-C1">(A) Our adoption
    donations go towards covering the expenses incurred in rescuing your pet.Our fees,

    your vet has completed the surgery, send us a copy of your receipt along with the
    form included in your adoption agreement. You will then have the option to have your
    deposit returned or you can help other animals in need by donating it. </span></p>
<p class="Default-Paragraph-Font-P"><span class="Normal-C1"><br>Almost Home is a non-<wbr>profit organization which receives no federal funding. We rely
    solely on private donations and adoption fees. Your donation helps us continue our

    animals.<br><br></span><span class="Normal-C0">8. (Q) How do I contact Almost Home?<br></span><span class="Normal-C1">(A) By e-<wbr>mail at <a href="mailto:almosthome1965@gmail.com">almosthome1965@gmail.com</a>.

    in a timely manner.<br><br></span><span class="Normal-C0">9.(Q) How do I show off my foster-<wbr>to-<wbr>adopt pet?</span></p>
