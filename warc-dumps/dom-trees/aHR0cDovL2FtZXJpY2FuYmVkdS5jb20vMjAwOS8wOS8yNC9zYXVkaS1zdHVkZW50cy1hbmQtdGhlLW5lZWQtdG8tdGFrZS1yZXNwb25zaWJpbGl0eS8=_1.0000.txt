				<h2><a href="http://americanbedu.com/2009/09/24/saudi-students-and-the-need-to-take-responsibility/" rel="bookmark" title="Saudi Students and the Need to Take&nbsp;Responsibility">Saudi Students and the Need to Take&nbsp;Responsibility</a></h2>

				<div class="postinfo">
Posted on <span class="postdate">September 24, 2009</span> by American Bedu	
				</div>

				<div class="entry">

					<div class="snap_preview"><div class="pd-rating" id="pd_rating_holder_155549_post_4309"></div>
<p><script type="text/javascript" charset="utf-8"><!--//--><![CDATA[//><!--
PDRTJS_settings_155549_post_4309={"id":155549,"unique_id":"wp-post-4309","title":"Saudi Students and the Need to Take Responsibility","permalink":"http:\/\/americanbedu.com\/2009\/09\/24\/saudi-students-and-the-need-to-take-responsibility\/","item_id":"_post_4309"}
//--><!]]&gt;</script><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-4310" title="school photo" src="http://delhi4cats.files.wordpress.com/2009/09/school-photo.jpg?w=468&amp;h=350" alt="school photo" width="468" height="350"></p>
<p>American President Barack Obama gave a compelling <a href="http://www.latimes.com/news/nationworld/nation/la-na-obama-schools8-2009sep09,0,3739220.story">speech</a> to the students of America as they began their new school term.&nbsp; He shared with these students on the need to be good students and how their contributions from when they were students would make a difference in the future outcome of the United States.&nbsp; He acknowledged some of the problems and pressures that American students can face and related some of his own experiences to illustrate his understanding.&nbsp; He further noted the responsibilities of parents, teachers and the government in helping with education.&nbsp; However the main emphasis I believe he sought to convey to the students was their need to take responsibility for their education and in turn for making a positive difference and contribution to their country.</p>
<p>This speech has me thinking about the Saudi education system.&nbsp; Overall I think the Saudi education system is good in providing the tools and resources to its students.&nbsp; In the Saudi system, every child has the right of an education and not only a primary or secondary education but the right to a University education too and at the expense of the Saudi government.&nbsp; Saudi students who are considering a bachelor, master or doctorate degree have the choices of seeking such higher education from within Saudi Arabia or from outside of Saudi Arabia.&nbsp; The students are awarded scholarships for their education from the Saudi government which provides for all the students needs such as tuition, ability to acquire necessary books, living expenses if required and a regular monthly stipend.&nbsp; Saudi students outside of the Kingdom also receive medical benefits.&nbsp; As I remarked earlier, I think this is a good system for building an educated body but I have some other speculations too.</p>
<p>Because these education benefits are considered as ‘due rights’ of a Saudi student, how many of the students really appreciate this gift the Saudi government provides to them?&nbsp; How many of these students take advantage of the education opportunity to obtain another “check mark” in making one more marriageable or as the opportunity to live outside the Kingdom?&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; How many of the students look upon this gift from the Saudi government as something which they should in turn give back?&nbsp; How much pride is there among Saudi students to study hard for the future of their country and the opportunity to make a difference?
