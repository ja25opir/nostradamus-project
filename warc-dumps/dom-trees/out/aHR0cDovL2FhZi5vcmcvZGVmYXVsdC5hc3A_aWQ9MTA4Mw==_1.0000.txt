                                <h2 align="left">December 24, 2009<br>
<br>
To: All  AAF Members<br>
From: Clark  Rector, Executive Vice President-Government Affairs<br>
Re: Healthcare Passed â€“ Advertising Deductibility  Safe!</h2>
<br>
<p align="left">On  Christmas Eve, after a long and protracted struggle, the U.S. Senate passed its  version of healthcare reform.&nbsp; <strong>The final bill as passed did not include  any language denying the federal tax deductibility for the advertising of  prescription drugs or any other product.</strong></p>
<p align="left">
  This was  one of the most serious threats the advertising industry has faced in many  years.&nbsp; As you know from my frequent  emails over the past months, this has not been an easy fight.&nbsp; Limiting the deductibility of pharmaceutical  advertising was discussed at virtually every step of the process, from the  House Ways and Means Committee, to the Senate Finance Committee and finally  through threatened amendments on the Senate floor.
  </p>
<p align="left">
  The fact  that the proposal was never brought to a vote is due in very large part to the  outstanding grassroots response of the advertising industry.&nbsp; We know from our contacts with lawmakers on  both sides of the Capitol that they heard loud and clear from you and other  industry professionals that limiting advertising deductibility would be a  disastrous choice, both economically and politically.
  </p>
<p align="left">
  We know  that this idea will not go away and its proponents are likely try again in the  future.&nbsp; In the meantime, thanks to all  of you for such an outstanding response throughout the year and congratulations  on an outstanding victory.</p>
  <p align="left">
  As  always, do not hesitate to contact me if you have any comments or questions.</p>
