                    <tr>
                        <td colspan="2" valign="top">
                            <div class="msgtext">It’s been great to hear about the good times, had by all the privileged students that have been to the Jansen’s for seminars.  <br>
<br>
Well I (and two friends from the Guild) were privileged to be invited to paint at the Dawbon Art Studio.  Perhaps they are related to the Jansen’s?  Certainly as warm and hospitable.  This includes the entire family.  <br>
<br>
Our 2 hour class became an entire day of visiting, laughter, pranks with the class at Jansen’s (via Skype), and a pizza party to top it off.  Blake and Georgia’s reasoning, ‘why drive home in rush hour?  You might as well stay till after it’.  Sounded like good advice to us, especially with their two kids home from school.  That was an extra privilege.....meeting Hale and Rachel.  <br>
<br>
We didn’t get to spend as much time with Hale, as he and his Dad were off to the performing arts of ‘Garage Band’ or some other similar entertainment.....yet we heard them!  They were great!  <br>
<br>
Let me tell you about Rachel.  She is as comfortable in the studio as her Mom.  Although quite young still, I’m sure we will see a lot from her (in the future) as she is an artist in the making.  Can you guess her favourite subject?  Art of course!  A well-versed and interesting young woman.  We all enjoyed her company very much.  (I would love to have taken her home with me!)  <br>
Both kids are a testament to the fine parenting skills of Blake and Georgia.  <br>
<br>
My two traveling companions are not on the forum yet, so I will convey their experiences;<br>
<br>
“It was such a great painting day yesterday at the Dawbon’s.....Georgia is such a lovely person and teacher.  I will master these new paints yet,”  <br>
“I have never been so relaxed in a class especially considering it was using a new technique and new paints. &nbsp;I have renewed energy for painting and look forward to really mastering this technique. &nbsp;Georgia was a great teacher. &nbsp;I'm so glad we could all afford to stay late and do more painting.”<br>
<br>
For myself, I ordered the Direct Painting series when it first came out last year.  I did the first painting of the white flower.....and struggled with the technique, so I put it away, and began working on other unfinished projects.  One good thing happened - I was at least painting on a regular basis now.<br>
<br>
The invite to Georgia’s class has catapulted me back to the direct painting.  Georgia is a skilled and very relaxed teacher.  At the beginning of class, we all ‘got it’.  We understood and were able to execute the technique.  As time passed and we got a bit tired, old habits were creeping back in.  The neat part was recognizing this.  I now know I WILL be able to complete the direct painting series.  Thank you Georgia!<br>
I haven’t been able to pick up the brush since I’ve been home (the last two days).  Yet I have pondered what I learned from Georgia, and from you on the forum.  My next painting plan will be to set a timer for small bursts of time when beginning direct painting.    When I start to blend, I will walk away from it until my head clears.  I think we need to be ‘fresh’ when painting this style.  <br>
<br>
A happy painter,<br>
Carol MacDonald</div>
