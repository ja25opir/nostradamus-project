                        <div class="message">
                            <p>By Steve Wiltfong
</p><p>Avon (Ind.) High outside linebacker Armonze Daniel is still looking to earn a few more scholarship offers, and has one school in mind.
</p><p>“Auburn was one of my top schools, even before the season started,” Daniel said. “I like how they play. They’re definitely very aggressive and they love playmakers. They’re in the SEC and I want to play down there. I love it down there and I grew up down there.”
</p><p>This week, the 6-foot-4, 225-pound Daniel plans to send Auburn his senior film through four games. Several other schools have already seen enough clips of the Hoosier State standout, to extend him an offer. Daniel holds over 10 full-rides from the likes of Cincinnati, Illinois, Indiana, Iowa, Iowa State, Minnesota and Purdue.
</p><p>“Hopefully (Auburn) sees something they like and I get an offer.”
</p><p>In the meantime, Daniel and his parents, who still live in his hometown of Tuscaloosa, keep in regular touch with Tigers defensive coordinator/linebackers coach Ted Roof.
</p><p>While Daniel patiently waits on word from Auburn, he’s planned an official visit to Iowa, and is also hearing a lot from Michigan. He’ll return to the Hawkeyes campus for the Penn State game on the weekend of Oct. 2nd.
</p><p>“Iowa, I just like how they’re a family,” Daniel said. “Everything is together out there. The coaching staff is really dedicated to wining. I hate losing. I have a good relationship with coach (Darrell) Wilson.”
</p><p>Daniel plans to take his other four official visits following his senior season, with a final decision coming very late in the process. In the past, he’s taken trips to Alabama, Mississippi State, LSU, Georgia, Iowa, Indiana and Purdue.
</p><p>“They have to win,” Daniel stated of what he’s looking for in a school. “They have to be very productive with their players. I want to play in the NFL someday, that’s always been my dream. I know a lot of people say that’s the dream, but this is what I live. Football is my life.  After that, I have to have a good relationship with the coaches. I want to go to as many bowl games as I can while I’m in college, and enjoy the atmosphere wherever I go to school.”
</p><p>In just four games as a junior, Daniel racked up over 60 tackles.</p>
