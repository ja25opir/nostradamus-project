				<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://afamiliarpath.com/2010/05/too-busy/" title="Too busy" rel="bookmark">Too busy</a></h2>
		
		<div class="post-info">
				<span class="time published" title="2010-05-21T00:56:01+0000">May 21, 2010</span> <span class="author vcard">By <a href="http://afamiliarpath.com/author/admin/" title="Posts by Melissa" class="url fn" rel="nofollow">Melissa</a></span> <span class="categories">Filed Under <a href="http://afamiliarpath.com/category/daily-life/" title="View all posts in daily life" rel="category tag">daily life</a></span> 
				</div>

		<div class="entry-content">
			<p>I hate it when my favorite bloggers are too busy to blog. I hate it when they tell me they are too busy to blog, because if they are too busy, they are surely doing something fun and exciting that I’d want to know about. Don’t you think?</p>
<p><a title="blanket" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/35248265@N04/4626161882/"><img alt="blanket" src="http://static.flickr.com/4030/4626161882_38d4cef454.jpg" border="0"></a></p>
<p>That’s why I’m not going to tell you that I’m too busy to blog (even though I am), because then you’ll wonder about all the exciting things going on around here and I’m afraid you’d be let down. But here are a few of the busy things that happened this week:</p>
<p>1. Two weeks ago I declared we were having a yard sale this Friday and it very quickly snuck up on me. I was not ready. I spent a few hours late this evening getting things out and decided to do it the Dave Ramsey way, not price anything. I’ll let you know how that goes.</p>
<p>2. I took some pictures of <a href="http://thejoyfulchaos.com">Suzanne’s</a> kids and let me tell you what, they are adorable! I can’t wait to show you her chubby little boy. He’s delicious. </p>
<p>3. Earlier this week we had a visit from some homeschool friends and that same day I had to rush Mr. Darcy to the vet. He was crying in pain from his ears. He is the dog that cannot ride in the car, so I took out a van seat and put in the big pet carrier cage we have. I thought that would solve the problem. When I got home I had to vacuum, steam clean and fumigate the carpet, then I had to bathe the filthy dog. Multiple instances of vomit and poo cannot be contained inside a wire pet carrier in a moving vehicle. Just for future reference.</p>
<p>All that and he just had a few ticks in his ear. </p>
<p>4. There were some other things like school, a trip to Walmart, church and laundry that occupied me too. </p>
<p>Now you’re all caught up. And I managed to squeeze all that in before midnight!</p>

				<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://afamiliarpath.com/2010/05/season-finale/" title="Season finale" rel="bookmark">Season finale</a></h2>
		
		<div class="post-info">
				<span class="time published" title="2010-05-19T11:59:21+0000">May 19, 2010</span> <span class="author vcard">By <a href="http://afamiliarpath.com/author/admin/" title="Posts by Melissa" class="url fn" rel="nofollow">Melissa</a></span> <span class="categories">Filed Under <a href="http://afamiliarpath.com/category/blogging/" title="View all posts in blogging" rel="category tag">blogging</a></span> 
				</div>

		<div class="entry-content">
			<p>What if blogs had season finales?</p>
<p>What would be the cliffhanger? Would this blog even be coming back next year, or due to low ratings would I be booted off the air? Maybe moved to a new time slot.</p>
<p>I won’t ask for a vote. I might not like the results. </p>
<p>I’m sad about the Lost finale. I am happy to be getting some answers to this mysterious show, but just one more show left is too hard to even think about. </p>
<p><a title="004" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/35248265@N04/4617218965/"><img alt="004" src="http://static.flickr.com/4033/4617218965_7199f6aa2a.jpg" border="0"></a></p>
<p>Are the end of television shows ever really satisfactory? Newhart? Awful finale. Seinfeld? I hated the last show. Friends? That one was actually pretty good. </p>
<p>Do you watch Lost? Are you ready for The End?</p>

				<h2 class="entry-title"><a href="http://afamiliarpath.com/2010/05/it-feels-like-monday/" title="It feels like Monday" rel="bookmark">It feels like Monday</a></h2>
		
		<div class="post-info">
				<span class="time published" title="2010-05-18T00:11:27+0000">May 18, 2010</span> <span class="author vcard">By <a href="http://afamiliarpath.com/author/admin/" title="Posts by Melissa" class="url fn" rel="nofollow">Melissa</a></span> <span class="categories">Filed Under <a href="http://afamiliarpath.com/category/daily-life/" title="View all posts in daily life" rel="category tag">daily life</a>, <a href="http://afamiliarpath.com/category/homeschool/" title="View all posts in homeschool" rel="category tag">homeschool</a></span> 
				</div>

		<div class="entry-content">
			<p>My girls started early this morning with the sister fights. By this evening they could have had their own Real Housewives of New York show. I’m trying to be the neutral, noncommittal camera man. Not that I’m filming any of it. This is not something I want to watch again in the future. I’m just trying to stay out of it, no matter how many times they come to me trying to gain my sympathy. </p>
<p>“Would you pray that Clementine is nicer to me tomorrow,” was the bedtime plea that started it all up again because Clementine overheard that comment and was not pleased to be prayed for.</p>
<p>It’s hard to be a sister. I remember. My sister and I had monumental fights over simple things like unloading the dishwasher. It’s probably why she moved over an hour away when she grew up. </p>
<p>Speaking of my sister, she visited today and she had to put my kids in time out. For splashing her at the pool. What did she expect? Both things made me smile. Them splashing her, her putting them in time out. </p>
<p><a title="big dog" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/35248265@N04/4617834322/"><img alt="big dog" src="http://static.flickr.com/3404/4617834322_27e63c4abc.jpg" border="0"></a></p>
<p>That’s my aunt’s huge dog. Tess was giving him a kiss. This was still the weekend, when things were happy and light.</p>
<p>During Monday’s school work, you’ll find me pulling my hair out. My son holds his pencil wrong. Oh, why does kindergarten have to be so torturous? If I were to send my kids to real school just one year it would be the year they learn to read and write. My insides crawl when I have to listen to him sound out the words in <em>Teach Your Child to Read in 100 Easy Lessons</em>. They could have left out the easy part in the title. It would have been more believable. </p>
<p>During read aloud time he bounces off the couch and taps things annoyingly on the table until I scream. </p>
<p><a title="handwriting" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/35248265@N04/4617219051/"><img alt="handwriting" src="http://static.flickr.com/4059/4617219051_5ec25d5e8d.jpg" border="0"></a></p>
<p>Making him hold his pencil correctly causes spasms and fits. I have this neat little guide that helps kids hold their pencils right. He hates using it.</p>
<p>(Fits not pictured.)</p>
<p><a title="handwriting 2" href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/35248265@N04/4617219139/"><img alt="handwriting 2" src="http://static.flickr.com/4062/4617219139_7faab6c7d0.jpg" border="0"></a></p>
<p>But it does work.</p>
<p>If only Mario Kart Wii taught him reading and writing, what a beautiful world it would be.</p>
<p>Help me Lord, I have one more child to teach reading and writing. </p>
