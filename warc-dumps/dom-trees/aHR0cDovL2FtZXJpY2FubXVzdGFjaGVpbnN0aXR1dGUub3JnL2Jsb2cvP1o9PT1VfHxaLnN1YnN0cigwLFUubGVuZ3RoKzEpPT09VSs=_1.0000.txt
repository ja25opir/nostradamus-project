				<h2><a href="http://americanmustacheinstitute.org/blog/2010/09/soccer-mustaches-could-net-a-year-of-free-hooters-wings/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Soccer Mustaches Could Net A Year of Free Hooters Wings">Soccer Mustaches Could Net A Year of Free Hooters Wings</a></h2>
				<small>September 15th, 2010 <!-- by DrAbeFroman --></small>

				<div class="entry">
					<p>The Mustached American people have not historically been “soccer people,” generally because we tend to gravitate to more physically brutal sports such as mixed martial arts, <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JA8uYUn7LPc" target="_blank">midget wrestling</a>, cock fighting, and fisting.<a href="http://americanmustacheinstitute.org/wp-content/uploads/2010/09/Soccer-Mustache1.jpg"><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-747" title="Soccer Mustache1" src="http://americanmustacheinstitute.org/wp-content/uploads/2010/09/Soccer-Mustache1-199x300.jpg" alt="" width="199" height="300"></a></p>
<p>However, as Malcolm X demonstrated, people must grow and expand their intellectual paradigms.</p>
<p>That is why the American Mustache Institute has built a bridge with the soccer community, creating a partnership with St. Louis soccer club <a href="http://www.ac-stlouis.com/" target="_blank">AC St. Louis</a> and Hooters.</p>
<p>The partnership will kick off  this Saturday, Sept. 18, at 7pm, with an AMI sponsored <em>Mustache Night</em> at AC St. Louis’s home field, followed by a post-game, mustache- and Hooters girls-heavy party at <a href="http://www.myspace.com/marylandheightshooters" target="_blank">Hooters in nearby Maryland Heights</a>.</p>
<p>Here’s how it works:</p>
<ul>
<li>All fans with a real or fake mustache and who purchase $5 parking <span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>get in to the game free</strong></span>.</li>
<li>At halftime there will be a “Best Mustache” judging by the lovely ladies of Hooters.</li>
<li>The winner of “Best Mustache” wins Hooters wings for free, which does not suck.</li>
</ul>
<p>It’s as simple, delicious, and flavor-saving as that. Any questions about this immense promotion should be directed to Bradley Cleveland, Director of Ticket Operations for AC StLouis at bcleveland@ac-stlouis.com or 618.798.0127.</p>
<p>Carry on.</p>

				<h2><a href="http://americanmustacheinstitute.org/blog/2010/09/dreams-of-mustached-mascot-of-the-year/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Dreams of Mustached Mascot of the Year">Dreams of Mustached Mascot of the Year</a></h2>
				<small>September 8th, 2010 <!-- by DrAbeFroman --></small>

				<div class="entry">
					<p>I’m a dreamer. And much like our fascination for Mr. T causes me to repeatedly dream I’m his sidekick in “A-Team” episodes, I’ve always dreamed of being a mascot. To be large, fluffy, and with a permanent expression no matter what the situation – cool under pressure akin to Chuck Norris even when surrounded by a troupe of Lithuanian assassins with eye patches. It doesn’t get much better.</p>
<p></p><div id="attachment_739" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 180px"><a href="http://americanmustacheinstitute.org/wp-content/uploads/2010/09/Paydirt-Pete.jpg"><img class="size-full wp-image-739" title="Paydirt-Pete" src="http://americanmustacheinstitute.org/wp-content/uploads/2010/09/Paydirt-Pete.jpg" alt="" width="170" height="300"></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Paydirt Pete</p></div><p></p>
<p>Despite this fascination, the Mustached American community has some mild concerns about the premier mascot showcase — the <a href="http://www.capitalonebowl.com/" target="_blank">Capital One Mascot Championship </a>– concerns that are being discussed with willing, open-minded officials at the highest levels of Capital One, as well as with the <a href="http://www.unitedmascots.com/" target="_blank">United Coalition of College Mascots</a>.</p>
<p>While this year’s Capital One <a href="http://www.capitalonebowl.com/vote/main" target="_blank">All-America Mascot Team</a> is broader than ever and ranges from the glitz of UCLA’s Joe Bruin to the mononymous Monte of the University of Montana, just two of the 16-mascot-team have mustaches: the University of Idaho’s <a href="http://www.myspace.com/uijoevandal" target="_blank">Joe Vandal</a> and the University of Texas El Paso’s Paydirt Pete.&nbsp; That’s only 12.5 percent.</p>
<p>So as the mascot team competes throughout the college football season to be named the Capital One National Mascot of the Year at the Capital One Bowl on Jan. 1, winning $15,000 for their university mascot scholarship program – it will serve as a reminder of 1988, when mustache penetration in America was at an all-time low (ironically also about 12 percent).</p>
<p>But we will indeed encourage <a href="http://www.capitalonebowl.com/vote/main" target="_blank">voting each week,</a> supporting the candidacies of Idaho’s Mr. Vandal and UTEP’s Mr. Pete, as well as encouraging write-ins for the likes of New Mexico State’s Pistol Pete, Virginia’s Cavalier, Wyoming’s Cowboy Joe, and of course, Three Rivers Community College’s <a href="http://www.trcc.edu/athletics/" target="_blank">Rocky Raider</a>.</p>
<p>Can a Mustached American win the Capital One National Mascot of the Year? I can only dream.</p>

				<h2><a href="http://americanmustacheinstitute.org/blog/2010/08/portland-doctor-fights-cancer-with-his-mustache/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Portland Doctor Fights Cancer With His Mustache">Portland Doctor Fights Cancer With His Mustache</a></h2>
				<small>August 12th, 2010 <!-- by DrAbeFroman --></small>

				<div class="entry">
					<p>After both of his parents were diagnosed with cancer, <a href="http://www.ahimsahealth.com/index.html" target="_blank">Dr. Lou Jacobs</a> decided to fight cancer with his mustache.</p>
<p><a href="http://americanmustacheinstitute.org/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/Screen-shot-2010-08-12-at-12.24.53-PM.png"><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-696" title="Screen shot 2010-08-12 at 12.24.53 PM" src="http://americanmustacheinstitute.org/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/Screen-shot-2010-08-12-at-12.24.53-PM-275x300.png" alt="" width="241" height="262"></a>The Portland, Maine-based chiropractic acupuncturist’s mother had passed away in May 2010 after battling inflammatory breast cancer and his father is scheduled for a bone marrow transplant for leukemia on August 25.</p>
<p>So Dr. Lou is working to raise upwards of $10,000 for cancer research and education by January 1, 2011, for which he will shave off his luxurious lip sweater — if the voters want him to do so.</p>
<p>With each donation, Dr. Lou will take votes — save or shave.</p>
<p>Dr. Jacobs has partnered with Roger Inhorn, M.D., Ph.D., medical director of oncology and hematology at Mercy Hospital in Portland, who will help fundraise and the two are working to secure a monthly spot on local news to discuss cancer detection, prevention and treatment.</p>
<p>For more information, contact Dr. Lou Jacobs in his office at (207) 774-6251, mobile at (207) 807-1119, or email <a onclick="top.Popup.composeWindow('pcompose.php?sendto=drj%40drloujacobs.com'); return false;" href="mailto:drj@drloujacobs.com" target="_blank">drj@drloujacobs.com.</a></p>

			<div class="post-657 post hentry category-uncategorized tag-ami tag-curtis-berger tag-moustache tag-mustache tag-scholarship tag-school tag-tuition" id="post-657">
				<h2><a href="http://americanmustacheinstitute.org/blog/2010/08/south-florida-chapter-presents-2nd-annual-ami-scholarship/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to South Florida Chapter Presents 2nd Annual AMI Scholarship">South Florida Chapter Presents 2nd Annual AMI Scholarship</a></h2>
				<small>August 5th, 2010 <!-- by DrAbeFroman --></small>

				<div class="entry">
					<p>The American Mustache Institute South Florida Chapter has awarded its 2<sup>nd</sup> annual American Mustache Institute Scholarship to Curtis Berger, a senior at Jensen Beach High School in Jensen Beach, Fla.<a href="http://americanmustacheinstitute.org/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/Heinz.jpg"><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-659" title="Heinz" src="http://americanmustacheinstitute.org/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/Heinz-300x225.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="225"></a></p>
<p>The annual scholarship, created by AMI South Florida Bureau Chief &amp; President Hans Heinz, is given each year to a deserving senior who can grow a thick and luxurious lip cabana, and vows to uphold all things that are, as the Dutch say, “mustachey.”</p>
<p>The scholarship contest is open to men and women who will graduate in the calendar year, and judged by AMI’s Heinz and a blue ribbon panel of Mustached American culture experts.</p>
<p>The winner of the honor is given a certificate of achievement, a photograph with AMI’s Heinz, a copy of Hall &amp; Oates’ Greatest Hits, &nbsp;and of course, the scholarship totaling $5 which can be used toward tuition, room and board, books, or to purchase films featuring midgets.</p>
<p>“Never has there been a more deserving recipient of this award,” said Heinz, who’s panel chose the winner among a field of twenty seniors this year.&nbsp; “The scholarship keeps gaining speed and popularity and I hope to see this thing go national in the future.”</p>
<p>Berger, as expected, was humbled by the experience and honor.</p>
<p>“It is really icing on the cake,” he said. “I had already received a full ride to Tusculum University on a soccer scholarship, which was due partially to me growing a mustache for my junior and senior years.&nbsp; The stache greatly enhanced my athleticism and my overall manliness.&nbsp; Besides that, it got me lot’s of chicks.”</p>
<p>When asked what he planned to do with his scholarship money, Berger added, “I plan to use the money to get myself a Double Down from KFC!”</p>
<p>Well played Curtis… well played.</p>
<p>Carry on.</p>

				<h2><a href="http://americanmustacheinstitute.org/blog/2010/08/ami-calls-for-protest-of-inoko-japanese-steakhouse-in-athens-georgia/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to AMI Calls for Protest of Inoko Japanese Steakhouse In Athens, Georgia">AMI Calls for Protest of Inoko Japanese Steakhouse In Athens, Georgia</a></h2>
				<small>August 4th, 2010 <!-- by DrAbeFroman --></small>

				<div class="entry">
					<p>Tyler Dewitt is like any other American young man. He is a 21-year-old history and finance major at the University of Georgia, working his way through school as a waiter at the <a href="http://www.facebook.com/pages/Inoko-Japanese-Steakhouse-on-Alps-Road/91359101598" target="_blank">Inoko Japanese Steakhouse</a> in Athens, Georgia; as well as being a robust ping pong player with a penchant for Hardee’s Monster Thickburgers and disabled horses.</p>
<p><a href="http://americanmustacheinstitute.org/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/TylerAMI.jpg"><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-645" title="TylerAMI" src="http://americanmustacheinstitute.org/wp-content/uploads/2010/08/TylerAMI-e1280933846728-225x300.jpg" alt="" width="225" height="300"></a>Most important, however, Mr. Dewitt is a proud, unflinching Mustached American, and it is because of that heritage that his employers at Inoko have chosen to discriminate against him and his heritage.</p>
<p>“For the past few months, Tyler has been working at Inoko while cultivating a handlebar mustache,” said his Senior Mustache Advocate, Stephen Hancock. “Until recently the two have coexisted peacefully. Now management has decided they want him to get rid of his mustache.”</p>
<p>According to Mssrs. Dewitt and Hancock, an incident recently occurred involving a hair in a salad, which was then used as leverage to demand Mr. Dewitt shave his mustache. He was told that if he did not comply, he would be terminated.</p>
<p>Clearly, internment camps for Japanese Americans following the Pearl Harbor attack has done nothing to teach the owners of Inoko about the terrors of discrimination.</p>
<p>Mr. Dewitt works his next shift is Saturday, August 7, and intends to arrive and work with his mustache held high.&nbsp; The American Mustache Institute asks that you support Mr. Dewitt by joining him at the restaurant with signs protesting these discriminatory practices, as well as visiting <a href="http://www.facebook.com/pages/Inoko-Japanese-Steakhouse-on-Alps-Road/91359101598?v=wall" target="_blank">Inoko’s Facebook page</a> and leaving comments voicing your displeasure.</p>
<p>You can also reach Mr. Dewitt at (404) 717-3041</p>
<p>Carry on.</p>
				</div>
