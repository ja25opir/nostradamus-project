    <td><p><b><font face="Arial,Helvetica"><font color="#000000"><font size="+2">SHIP
LISTS</font></font></font></b> <br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; The major ship lists that have survived from
this period are the lists of the seven ships that brought Acadians from
France to Louisiana in 1785, so that material will be covered in <b><a href="gencaj1.htm">Cajun
Genealogy</a></b>.&nbsp; To put it briefly, the material has appeared in
3 different books, and it can also be found at this website on the <b><a href="7ships.htm">7
ships</a></b> page. <br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Lists of Acadians
who migrated to France from England in <font color="#000000">1763 can be
found in <b><i>The Acadians in France, Vol. 2</i></b> (Milton &amp; Norma
Reider).&nbsp; Francois Roux has the lists from 4 ships [</font><a href="http://perso.wanadoo.fr/froux/divers/ambition.html" target="new">Ambition</a>, <a href="http://perso.wanadoo.fr/froux/divers/dorothee.html" target="new">Dorothee</a>, <a href="http://perso.wanadoo.fr/froux/divers/fauvette.html" target="new">Fauvette</a>, <a href="http://perso.wanadoo.fr/froux/divers/esturge.html" target="new">l'Esturgeon</a>.<font color="#000000">]
that brought Acadians from England to France in 1763.</font> <br>
<font color="#000000">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; I've
read that there were some lists made of the Acadians being deported, but
they were lost or destroyed.&nbsp; It seems that the Acadians were treated
more like cattle than people.&nbsp; There are no lists of the 1758 exile,
though you may be able to reconstruct some information from the data once
they got to France.&nbsp; I'll see if I can work on this in the future.&nbsp;
I hear that one of the side benefits of <a href="white.htm">Stephen White</a>'s <b><i><a href="dictionnaire.htm">Dictionnaire</a></i></b> is that he may be able to come up with a partial ship list of the <i>Duke
William</i> and <i>Violet</i> (both sank en route) based on people in the
deported communities who disappeared after 1758.&nbsp; As for their movements
during the exile, there are no ship lists as such.&nbsp; \</font> <br>
<font color="#000000">&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; There is a short list
of the <i>Brigite</i>, a small schooner that brought a small group of Acadians
to Louisiana in 1788.&nbsp; Here it is: Anne Marguerite Babin, Charles
Babin, Francois Laurent Babin, Marie LeBlanc Babin, Mathurin Babin, Pierre
Moise Babin, Victoire Babin, Jean Baptiste Boudrot, Madeleine Bourg, Jean
Frederic Gravois, Jean Hubert Gravois, Joseph Gravois, Madeleine Blanche
Gravois, Marguerite Angelique Gravois, Marie Felicite Gravois, Marie Susanne
Gravois, Marie Tharsille Gravois, and Victoire Gravois.&nbsp;<font size="-1"> (<b><i>The Founding of New Acadia</i></b>, Brasseaux, p. 208)</font></font> </p>
