			<div class="content">Man that was alot of work. I know you can't see it all (especially with the parameters group) but it can now handle the full 167 user definable parameters of a poly-sprout attractor. What happenes is when you change the attractor type the group section for the parameters will change for each attractors needs. In the cases of say the biggies tabs are available to flip to the next page of parameters. In the cases of something like the IFS, and Poly-Sprout attractors and other in the future, a new drop down control is also there to allow the choice of degree matrix's or order's.
<br>Right now I have plans and think I have solved for adding in symmetry and projection mapping(sphereical, rectangular, polar, ect) and am hoping to have them in place pretty quick. However if it becomes a problem I'm going to hold of on those for the first release.
<br>Also note I have added a couple new envelopes as requested. Plus for the fun of it you can (or will soon) save your parameters out to a Chaoscope file.
<br>
<br>zuzzabuzz - true its being built for PC right now, but I'm trying my damndest to keep the code as platform independant as possible. Mind you though I have not played with anything related to Mac's since the Apple II days!!! There is an emulator though that should allow be to compile for Mac's that I intend on getting. If that goes south I'll hunt for someone to do the compile (and any conversions I may have missed) for Macs. But naturally thats down the road. Sorry my friend!
