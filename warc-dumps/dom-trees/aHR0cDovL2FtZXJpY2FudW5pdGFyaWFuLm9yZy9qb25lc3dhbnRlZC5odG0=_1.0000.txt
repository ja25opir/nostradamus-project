      <td width="81%">
        <p class="MsoTitle" style="word-spacing: 0; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="center"><b><font size="6">Wanted:
        An End to Dogmatic Religion</font></b></p>
        <b>
        <p style="word-spacing: 0; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="center">&nbsp;</p>
        <p style="word-spacing: 0; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="center"><font size="4"><span style="mso-bidi-font-size: 12.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: Times New Roman; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; mso-ansi-language: EN-US; mso-fareast-language: EN-US; mso-bidi-language: AR-SA">Steve
        Jones</span></font></p>
        <p style="word-spacing: 0; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="center"><span style="mso-bidi-font-size: 12.0pt; mso-fareast-font-family: Times New Roman; mso-bidi-font-family: Times New Roman; mso-ansi-language: EN-US; mso-fareast-language: EN-US; mso-bidi-language: AR-SA"><i><font size="3">Atlanta,

    <tr>
      <td width="19%"></td>
      <td width="81%" bgcolor="#ffffff">
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:
EN">Dogmatists think it their sworn duty to pass judgment on their brethren.
        They see it as a great commandment to spatter fellow Christians with the
        title "heretic," and that duty they discharge with all
        diligence. <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:
EN">&nbsp;<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:
EN">They also use such terms as "deceiver" and "false
        teacher," sometimes even “antichrist”, but their relationship
        with the word "heretic" is special. It echoes through the ages
        of religious bigotry. In a bygone era, the cry of "heretic"
        sent men and women to the flames and a host of unspeakable tortures at
        the hands of "the orthodox." It is only fitting, then, that
        today’s super-orthodox should have such an attachment to the word. <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:
EN">&nbsp;<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:
EN">The amazing thing is that the issues that distinguish the
        "heretics" from the "orthodox" are highly abstract
        with few, if any, practical applications.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">&nbsp;
        </span>Neither the precise natures of God and Christ, the dynamics of
        the atonement, nor the means of creation, produces the Christ-like
        goodness so essential to discipleship.<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:
EN">&nbsp;<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:
EN">Dogmatism convolutes the mission of Christ. Why did Jesus walk among sinners
        and preach his message? Was it to make men theologically astute? Did
        Jesus come chiefly to give us right metaphysics, to present dogmas to
        the mind? <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:
EN">&nbsp;<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:
EN">The doctrinal stalwart believes so, at least in part. To him, one of the
        great duties before God is to mark the right doctrinal box on the
        Christian checklist and to drive from the church all who don’t. The
        doctrine may have nothing to do with whether he loves God or neighbor,
        but to him, it is a matter of spiritual life and death. The dogmatist
        must expose all who deviate. He must ruin their reputations in the
        Christian community, and for this, he expects someday to hear the words,
        "Well done, good and faithful servant." <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:
EN">&nbsp;<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;font-style:italic;font-weight:bold;language:EN">‘False
        teachers’ and ‘heresies’<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">&nbsp; </span></span><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:EN"><o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:
EN">&nbsp;<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:
EN">Many heretic hunters will respond by quoting New Testament passages that
        condemn false teachers. There must, therefore, be such a thing, and it
        must be incumbent upon the church to find them and drag them into the
        light of day. So goes the reasoning. <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:
EN">&nbsp;<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:
EN">But the reasoning is flawed. Virtually every false teaching attacked in the
        New Testament is ethical in character. The biblical authors oppose these
        teachings because they led to either immoral living or harsh asceticism.
        The condemnation is not over the doctrine’s technical incorrectness,
        but over the consequences of belief in it. <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:
EN">&nbsp;<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:
EN">Gnosticism, for example, in one form taught that matter was unredeemable. It
        made no difference to Gnostics, therefore, what one did with the body.
        This led to sexual promiscuity and perversion. The early churches were
        infected with this teaching. It endangered Christian purity. The New
        Testament writers condemned it on these grounds. <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:
EN">&nbsp;<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:
EN">The Judaizing sects also caused problems in the church. They took the
        unfettered character of the faith and killed it. Austere Christians (as
        they do today) put heavy burdens on the saints. But Jesus never imposed
        any of these laws on his followers. Such things were never his emphasis.
        He knew they engendered only pride, an outward show of piety. This was
        the "other gospel" anathematized by Paul in Galatians. It was
        not an abstract belief about the nature of Jesus or the mechanics of
        salvation. This is a point the dogmatists always miss.<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:
EN">&nbsp;<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:
EN">Another is that false teachers of Paul's day openly withstood the apostles
        and led away disciples after themselves. They shouted down the very
        messengers that Christ had sent into the world. For this Paul, John and
        others issued stern rebuke. But honest dissenters from orthodoxy today
        (at least the responsible ones) do not rise up and demonize Paul. They,
        like their orthodox detractors, acknowledge the New Testament authors as
        authoritative. It is a matter of differing interpretation of the same
        data, not a flouting of apostolic authority. <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:
EN">&nbsp;<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:
EN">Still, the objection is common: The Bible enjoins us to follow sound
        doctrine, but the word "doctrine" simply means
        "teaching." We would really be better off if we discarded the
        old term "doctrine," for it is misleading. Many Christians
        think it means "abstract, theological concept," but there is
        no justification for this thinking. Any teaching is a doctrine. Jesus’
        statement "love your enemies" is a doctrine, a teaching. <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:
EN">&nbsp;<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:
EN">Another misunderstood word is "heresy." Look up the word in a
        concordance or a lexicon. You will find that it does not address
        doctrine per se, but only division. A heresy is anything that proves
        divisive. A heretic is one who ruins unity among brothers and sisters.
        Yes, false teaching can divide Christians, and thereby be a heresy, but
        so can true teaching and the insistence upon theological purity.
        Dogmatism is itself a heresy, maybe the worst. Few things have so
        severed the communion of saints. Few things have so pitted brother
        against brother, as William Ellery Channing so eloquently points out: <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in .4229in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify">&nbsp;</p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in .4229in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size: 12.0pt; language: EN">"</span><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:EN">It
        is truly wonderful, if excommunication for supposed error be the method
        of purifying the church, that the church has been so long and so
        woefully corrupted. Whatever may have been the deficiencies of
        Christians in other respects, they have certainly discovered no criminal
        reluctance in applying this instrument of purification. Could the
        thunders and lightnings of excommunication have corrected the atmosphere
        of the church, not one pestilential vapor would have loaded it for ages.
        The air of Paradise would not have been more pure, more refreshing. But
        what does history tell us? It tells us that the spirit of exclusion and
        denunciation has contributed more than all other causes to the
        corruption of the church, to the diffusion of error; and has rendered
        the records of the Christian community as black, as bloody, as revolting
        to humanity, as the records of empires founded on conquest and guilt."</span><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;
language:EN"><span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> [1] </span><o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;language:EN"><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">&nbsp;</span><o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;font-style:italic;font-weight:bold;
language:EN">What, then, must we believe?<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">&nbsp; </span><o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;font-style:italic;font-weight:bold;language:EN">&nbsp;<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;language:EN">But, says the detractor, how do we know what to
        believe unless we have an orthodoxy pointing the way? The answer is that
        only those things that are obviously Christian – part and parcel of
        the church’s witness from day one – should be deemed indispensable.
        These are the great themes that traverse the sacred scriptures from
        cover to cover, then the life of the church from the first century to
        the 21st. They shape the character into the image of Jesus. These, and
        only these, are the non-negotiables. <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;language:EN">&nbsp;<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;language:EN">I believe we must each decide for ourselves what
        these central tenets are. (The alternative is to let someone else decide
        for you.) I’ve come up with my own list of non-negotiables. They are
        small in number, but are believed by nearly all Christians in one form
        or another. Dogmatists complain that these things are "least-common
        denominators," but in today’s increasingly secular age, they may
        yet be revolutionary. <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;language:EN">&nbsp;<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;language:EN">I would include the following: <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:EN">*<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">&nbsp;
        </span>God is a Heavenly Father, characterized by love, goodness and
        justice. <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:EN">*<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">&nbsp;
        </span>Jesus bore a profound relation to God and exhibited the divine
        characteristics to a profound degree. <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:EN">*<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">&nbsp;
        </span>He was crucified as a supreme act of self-sacrifice. Three days
        later, his followers had an encounter with him that shook their lives
        and changed the world. <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:EN">*<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">&nbsp;
        </span>By the Holy Spirit, he bears a special relation to his people,
        who have found him to be a source of strength, healing, inner peace. <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:EN">*<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">&nbsp;
        </span>There is a future life. <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:EN">*<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">&nbsp;
        </span>Wickedness and righteousness will be recompensed. <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:EN">*<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">&nbsp;
        </span>God desires that we live in faith, peace, love, goodness,
        self-control, servant-hood. <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:EN">*<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">&nbsp;
        </span>Prayer is of great benefit. <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;language:EN">*<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">&nbsp;
        </span>There exists a family of saints, an intimate connection between
        members of the faith family. They meet regularly, become involved in
        each other’s lives, partake of the Lord’s Supper and practice
        baptism in one form or another. <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;language:EN"><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">&nbsp;</span><o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;language:EN">No, this is not a creed. Creeds are meant to
        condemn non-conformists. It is a personal interpretation of what is
        basic to Christian faith. If someone wants to omit baptism, for example,
        for his or her belief system, I won’t hurl insulting names at them or
        deny them the Christian name. I will simply realize that not all
        Christians see things as I do. <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;language:EN">&nbsp;<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;language:EN">My list omits doctrinal refinements, such the
        nature of God, the atonement, the meaning of inspiration. I believe
        these are side issues. To be openly dogmatic about them is to sow
        dissension and, thereby, become a true heretic. <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;language:EN">&nbsp;<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;language:EN">If we must adhere to a creed, let it be that
        ancient minimalist statement of faith, the Apostle's Creed. Here is a
        basic set of truths that most Christians can agree upon (give or take a
        few points). For large portions of Christendom, this sufficed for a long
        time. Not until Christ's people drank deeply from the well of Greek
        speculation did the arcane become binding. <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;language:EN">&nbsp;<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;font-style:italic;font-weight:bold;
language:EN">Conclusion </span><span style="font-size:12.0pt;line-height:100%;
font-style:italic;language:EN"><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">&nbsp;</span><o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;language:EN">&nbsp;<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;language:EN">The world is unimpressed with our erudite
        arguments. Our metaphysics seldom lay hold of the human heart. But when
        a group of social-minded Christians moves into a disaster area to fix
        houses, people notice. When a man accustomed to wild, self-centered
        living, adopts a Christian character, it astounds all who know him.
        Nobody cares whether he embraces one of the historic creeds. They see
        life, and that's all that matters to them. <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;language:EN">&nbsp;<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;language:EN">It is time, more than ever, to unite on the heart
        of Christian faith, to live it rather than demand conformity to
        somebody’s idea of "essentials," and to exalt the parable of
        the Good Samaritan above the churches’ most hallowed creeds and
        confessions. <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;language:EN">&nbsp;<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;language:EN">Dogmatism has had its day. Its chronicles reek of
        shed blood. Broken lives and relationships litter its halls. But a new
        day is here, theological bigotry must now be relegated to the dead past.
        <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;language:EN">&nbsp;<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;language:EN">Dogmatism needs a stake through its heart. <o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;language:EN">&nbsp;<o:p>
        </o:p>
        </span></p>
        <p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops: 0in; text-indent: 0; line-height: 100%; margin-left: 3; margin-right: 3; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 0" align="justify"><span style="font-size:12.0pt;
line-height:100%;language:EN">&nbsp;<o:p>
