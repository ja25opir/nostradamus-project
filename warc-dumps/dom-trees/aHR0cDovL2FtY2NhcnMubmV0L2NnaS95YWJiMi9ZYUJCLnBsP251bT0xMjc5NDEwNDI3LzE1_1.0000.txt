		<div class="message" style="float: left; width: 99%; overflow: auto;">
			1. There is no SR-4/T-5 V-8 bell, only a 6.5" deep six bell. The SR-4 was used in the 79 AMX with 304, but it used the six cylinder bell and trans. Marginal to say the least! 77-88 SR-4/T-5 bells work, the late 70s/mid 80s bells even have more than one trans pattern in them. &nbsp;<br> &nbsp;<br> 2. Early Falcons with the small sixes used a Borg-Warner T-96 just like the Rambler. You can get a 63+ Classic/64+ American 196 or 199 bell and buy or make an adapter. The earlier T-96 (and bell) had the bolts closer together top to bottom and won't work. Make sure you call and get the bolt pattern though -- I may have it backwards and you need the early bell. &nbsp;<br> &nbsp;<br> The adapter isn't cheap though -- $188. Scroll down to "Adapter plate, T-5, 60-65 Ford 140/170ci w/2.77 3-speed bellhousing, 83-93 T-5 trans." &nbsp; &nbsp;<br> <a href="http://www.safepay.net/cgi-bin/shop/cart.cgi?db=mddata.txt&amp;category=Adapters&amp;merchant=moderndriveline" target="_blank">http://www.safepay.net/cgi-bin/shop/cart.cgi?db=mddata.txt&amp;category=Adapters
&amp;merchant=moderndriveline</a> &nbsp;<br> &nbsp;<br> 3. Your biggest problem is the torque-tube. While it may be possible to make an adapter for the trans and shorten the tube, especially since you have a machine shop buddy, it is a lot of work. It's much easier for most to swap the rear axle. 93+ Ranger axles are within an inch or so of the same width as the Classic. The Explorer axle has been used, but is 1.5" wider than the Ranger. you can use the 7.5" or 8.8" axle. If keeping the six I'd use the 7.5", no point in carrying around a lot of unneeded weight! You'll have to build a four link setup, use ladder bars (32-36" bars actually ride fine on most roads, handles good too), or a "truck arm" setup (the HTH G-body kit would be easy to modify to fit -- <a href="http://www.hotrodstohell.net/truckarm/truckarm_gbody/truckarm_gbody.htm" target="_blank">http://www.hotrodstohell.net/truckarm/truckarm_gbody/truckarm_gbody.htm</a>). Then just have a driveshaft made. <br> &nbsp;<br> 4. With all involved, you might be better off to locate a 72+ 258 or even a 4.0L to install later. The 232 will be fine, but you can drive it a year or two while getting the new driveline ready. &nbsp;<br> &nbsp;<br> Some good links: &nbsp;<br> <a href="http://www.schwallers.com/t5swap.htm" target="_blank">http://www.schwallers.com/t5swap.htm</a> &nbsp;(64 American, but had V-8 and T-10 when swapped) <br> <a href="http://www.flatheadv8.org/ernie/ernie-t5.htm" target="_blank">http://www.flatheadv8.org/ernie/ernie-t5.htm</a> (lots of T-5 info, though nothing AMC specific)
