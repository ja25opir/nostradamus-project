		<h2>CARB</h2>
<div style="text-align: center"><img title="Presentaion of ARA/ARACA CARB Training Manual" height="298" alt="Presentaion of ARA/ARACA CARB Training Manual Photo" width="400" src="/UserFiles/image/Government/aracarb.jpg"><br>
<br>
&nbsp;</div>
<div>The California Air Resources Board is the state agency that is tasked with writing, implementing, and enforcing the majority of the air quality regulations passed by the California legislature.&nbsp;When the Federal Clean Air Act was passed in 1970, the Congress recognized that California had the nation’s most challenging air quality issues.&nbsp;As a result, California was granted extraordinary authority to regulate sources of pollution within their state.&nbsp;However, California’s regulatory really goes beyond the borders of their state because the Clean Air Act allows other states to adopt California regulations once the federal Environmental Protection Agency has reviewed and approved them.&nbsp;That means that the regulations California has placed on in-use diesel equipment could come to your state sometime in the future.&nbsp;This page of the ARA web contains a comprehensive archive of California regulations that potentially affect rental businesses as well as ARA’s comments on several regulatory actions that have been taken in recent years.</div>
<h3>California ARB Regulatory Update January 2010</h3>
<p>There are a number of issues, updates, and points of information that you need to know about California Air Resources Board regulations for off-road diesel equipment, on-road trucks and portable equipment. The following is an update on each regulation for your information.</p>
<p><strong>Off-road Equipment:</strong></p>
<p>The first compliance date for large fleets is March 1, 2010.</p>
<p>Current information regarding the off-road rule can be found at the following Web page.</p>
<p><a target="_blank" href="http://www.arb.ca.gov/msprog/ordiesel/ordiesel.htm">http://www.arb.ca.gov/msprog/ordiesel/ordiesel.htm</a></p>
<p>Over the past year, ARB and the ORIAG (industry implementation group) have developed and posted in-force regulatory requirements and a number of FAQs related to the regulation. You can view these at:</p>
<p><a target="_blank" href="http://www.arb.ca.gov/msprog/ordiesel/knowcenter.htm">http://www.arb.ca.gov/msprog/ordiesel/knowcenter.htm</a></p>
<p>There was a revision to the rule that was approved in July that offers compliance credits for certain fleet actions. There is a Web link that describes these credits.</p>
<p><a target="_blank" href="http://www.arb.ca.gov/msprog/ordiesel/faq/faq-early-credit.pdf">http://www.arb.ca.gov/msprog/ordiesel/faq/faq-early-credit.pdf</a></p>
<p><strong>On-road Trucks:</strong></p>
<p>The on-road truck rule was approved by the California Office of Administrative Law Dec. 9, 2009.</p>
<p>This regulation applies to 14,001 and heavier diesel GVWR vehicles that rental companies own or lease for either rental/leasing or servicing the business.</p>
<p>Synopsis of Rule <a target="_blank" href="http://www.arb.ca.gov/regact/2008/truckbus08/revfro.pdf">http://www.arb.ca.gov/regact/2008/truckbus08/revfro.pdf</a></p>
<p>The regulation applies to all fleets of one or more diesel vehicles over 14,000 GVWR. There are special provisions for fleets of three or fewer vehicles. All other fleets are subject to the large fleet requirements. The rule controls NO<sub>X</sub> and particulate emissions.</p>
<p>There are three compliance options. These are BACT (best available control technology), percentage BACT and averaging. For large fleets, the first compliance date is Jan. 1, 2011. Requirements become more stringent each year and are detailed in tables in the regulation. You may choose different methods for compliance for each emission and you may change compliance methods annually. Reporting is required if the fleet is certified using percentage BACT, averaging or if any compliance exemption is claimed. Generally there is no initial report; however, if you claim a vehicle retirement credit, you must make an early report by March 31, 2010.</p>
<p>Vehicles operated only in certain counties are exempted from NO<sub>X</sub> requirements. Also, vehicles operated below certain mileage thresholds are NO<sub>X</sub> exempt.</p>
<p>Particulate BACT is defined as either a 2007 model truck engine equipped with an OEM particulate filter or a California approved diesel particulate filter removing at least 85% of the particulate matter (called a level 3 VDECS). NO<sub>X</sub> BACT is an emission rate equal to that produced by a 2010 certified diesel engine.</p>
<p>All three options for compliance require that all vehicles in the fleet satisfy PM BACT or its equivalent emission beginning in 2014. Low use vehicles, that is vehicles operated for less than 100 hours and 1,000 miles per year, are not required to be included in fleet compliance demonstration. An exemption can also be obtained if no level 3 VDECS has been certified to retrofit the fleet vehicle in question. You may also substitute hybrid gasoline vehicles or alternative fueled vehicles (such as LPG and natural gas) into your fleet and comply under averaging even if you have not added a PM VDECS to every vehicle beginning in 2014.</p>
<p>Generally the regulation requires actions beginning Jan. 1, 2011. There are early retirement credits available if you shrink your fleet.&nbsp; Presently, you would have to report regarding such credits by March 31, 2010.</p>
<p>At the board hearing held on Dec. 9, 2009, the California Air Resources Board (ARB) directed staff to propose amendments to the Truck and Bus Regulation that would provide additional flexibility for fleets adversely affected by the economy. The board asked staff to evaluate alternatives that would meet emission targets and to focus on smaller businesses. The Board also asked staff to consider changing the March 31, 2010, reporting date to provide more time for fleets to submit their vehicle information. Staff is now preparing its analysis and will be drafting suggested amendments for review.&nbsp;Staff plans to hold public workshops in early 2010 to obtain stakeholder input.</p>
<p><strong>Portable Equipment Rule (PERP):</strong></p>
<p>ARB has published proposed modifications to the Portable Equipment Rule that could impact small rental companies. The hearing will be held Jan. 28, 2010, in Sacramento. The notice of meeting and regulatory proposal can be found at:</p>
<p><a target="_blank" href="http://www.arb.ca.gov/regact/2010/perp2010/perp2010.htm">http://www.arb.ca.gov/regact/2010/perp2010/perp2010.htm</a></p>
<p>ARB staff proposes to allow owners of small fleets to designate some of their currently registered engines to operate for one additional year beyond the current date.</p>
<ul>
    <li>Owners with 25 or fewer total portable engines would be able to continue operating either one spark-ignition engine of any size or up to five engines not to exceed a combined total of 500 brake horsepower (bhp) until Dec. 31, 2010.<br>
    &nbsp;</li>
    <li>Owners of 25 or fewer total portable engines would be able to choose either one diesel engine of any size or up to five diesel engines not to exceed a combined total of 500 bhp to operate until Dec. 31, 2010.</li>
</ul>
These engines would have to be currently registered in PERP or permitted by a local air district. Other modifications not generally affecting the rental industry have also been proposed. <br>
<br>
<h3>Attention ARA of California Rental Companies</h3>
<p>This is a reminder of the Aug. 1, 2009,&nbsp;deadline for all small fleets to report their off-road diesel vehicles to ARB.<br>
<br>
If you have entered your fleet data&nbsp;in DOORS, but not selected "Request Review," you must do so prior to 11:59 p.m. on Aug. 1, 2009, to meet&nbsp;the deadline. Large or medium fleets should have requested a review&nbsp;by April 1, 2009,&nbsp;and June 1, 2009, respectively, but&nbsp;if they have not, ARB staff encourages them to do so&nbsp;as soon as possible.&nbsp;<br>
<br>
Requesting a review sends your information to ARB staff. They will review the data and, if there are no significant errors apparent, assign Equipment Identification Numbers to your fleet.<br>
<br>
The option to request a review is on the second dropdown menu immediately after logging into DOORS. It is the very last option below "Enter fleet data online."<br>
<br>
If you have questions, please e-mail <a href="mailto:doors@arb.ca.gov">doors@arb.ca.gov</a> or call 877-59-DOORS.</p>
