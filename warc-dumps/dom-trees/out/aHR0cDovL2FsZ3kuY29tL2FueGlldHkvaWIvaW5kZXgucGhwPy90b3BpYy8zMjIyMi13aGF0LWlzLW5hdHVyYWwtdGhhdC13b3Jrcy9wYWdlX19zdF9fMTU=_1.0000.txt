I've found meditation helpful *BUT* if I'm already in an anxious state, meditation can make my mood worse. So I would recommend meditation for a time when you're already somewhat relaxed (I do it before bed, and it helps me enter sleep more quickly). You can experiment with different types of meditation, but I find for me leaving my eyes open and focussing visually on an object (a crystal, or a candle) is crucial. It helps me focus my mind and not get too into my head. And the deep breathing that is part of it= great for developing a calm mood. Again, though I think of this as more preventative than curative.<br>
<br>
I know that some nature food stores sell Kava tea, and Kava is also reported by some people to be very helpful against anxiety, although it didn't really seem to do much for me. I suspect it's the quantity issue again. And I tried it only once. I think it's harder to come by b/c there were claims made against it and liver toxicity, but it was never clear to me that it was any more dangerous than any of the benzos, for example. Anyway, you might want to look into it. <br>
<br>
Given that you just went off meds, my best advice would be that you come up with a regimen that seems right to you (vitamins, exercise, sleep, deep breathing, therapy-- therapy is crucial to me!!) and then also figure out what might help if your feelings start spiralling downward again. I find that just having a *plan* helps me a lot, so that if I know I'm going to experience anxiety again I have whatever I need in my cabinet. <br>
<br>
I've never been to a naturopath so I'd be curious to hear what anxious ppl.'s experiences have been...<br>
