New regulations from central bank to safeguard depositors

The Central Bank of Sri Lanka says that it has introduced a new insurance scheme to safeguard the deposits of people who deposit finances in Banks.

Accordingly if the bank the deposits have been placed in goes bankrupt the new insurance scheme will be utilized to provide relief to the depositors.

Speaking to GOLD FM News the Deputy Governor of the Central Bank Dharma Dheerasinghe stated that the relevant circular had already been issued to all the banks.

Meanwhile he also stated that this new insurance scheme will not come in to affect for all financial organisations which are registered at the Central Bank as yet.

However the Deputy Director of the Central Bank Dharma Dheerasinghe said that the Central Bank hopes to introduce this scheme to all financial organisations which are registered at the Central Bank in the future.
