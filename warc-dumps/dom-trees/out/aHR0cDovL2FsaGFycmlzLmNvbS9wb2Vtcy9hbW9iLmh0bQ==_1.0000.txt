<h4>Sanctuary Cove</h4>
<h6>Tucson, Arizona</h6>
<font size="1"><b>Reading by Alan Harris (<a href="../audio/sanctuary-cove.wma">wma</a> | <a href="../audio/sanctuary-cove.mp3">mp3</a>)</b></font><br><br>
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
Here is a chapel<br>
simple enough<br>
to welcome all creeds,<br>
all vegetation,<br>
all birds,<br>
all humans.<br>
<br>
People of vision built it up<br>
out of stone to serve souls<br>
upon this quiet foothill<br>
near Safford Peak.<br>
<br>
Visitors come for prayer<br>
or meditation or escape<br>
or inner alignment<br>
and enter into its peace.<br>
<br>
Not a myth, this place--<br>
mortared local stone,<br>
cactus needles fully sharp,<br>
red earth of ancient lava.<br>
<br>
When outer living has led<br>
to a thirst for contemplation,<br>
a path leads you to the door.<br>
<br>
Walk in.  Adjust your eyes.<br>
Choose a bench for sitting.<br>
Beliefs fade into Silence<br>
opening into Mystery<br>
as doves out on the roof<br>
are cooing a knowing<br>
that you lost long ago.<br>
</font>
</td></tr></tbody></table>

<a name="Wanting"><h4>Wanting</h4></a> 
<font size="1"><b>Reading by Alan Harris (<a href="../audio/wanting.wma">wma</a> | <a href="../audio/wanting.mp3">mp3</a>)</b></font><br><br>
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
I didn't want to have to want<br>
but I had to want not to hurt<br>
so I wanted what I felt was best<br>
but everyone else wanted it too<br>
and there wasn't enough of it<br>
so conflicts and hurt prevailed<br>
even though we wanted peace.<br>
<br>
Now what I really seem to want<br>
is not to have to want at all<br>
but if I can always never want<br>
will that be what I'll always want?<br>
</font>

<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
Plan on being<br>
warmer tomorrow<br>
with a 60 percent<br>
chance of light<br>
karma mixed<br>
with opportunity.<br>
<br>
No storms<br>
are in sight<br>
until Friday<br>
when a wave<br>
of retribution<br>

<br>
Your historical high<br>
for this date<br>
has been forgotten<br>
and let's not even<br>
think about your low.<br>
<br>
Tune in tomorrow,<br>
way in,<br>
and remember,<br>
if you don't have<br>
any weather,<br>
you are somewhere<br>
else.<br>

<a name="NowSweetNow"><h4>Now, Sweet Now</h4></a> 
<font size="1"><b>Reading by Alan Harris (<a href="../audio/now-sweet-now.wma">wma</a> | <a href="../audio/now-sweet-now.mp3">mp3</a>)</b></font><br><br>
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
When quiet has its way,<br>
a subtle glow may grow<br>
inside the heart's heart.<br>
<br>
One's furnishings reflect<br>
a different cast of light<br>
when silence fills the room.<br>
<br>
Consonance with core<br>
allows a laying down<br>
of petty weekday will.<br>
<br>
All cells become as servants<br>
to a Master higher than<br>
the calls of sense and self.<br>
<br>
True, jostlings and lacks<br>
and irritating chores<br>
await the coming down.<br>
<br>
Dark evil, multiform,<br>
may offer up its dirt,<br>
and errors their regret,<br>
<br>
but in this now, sweet now,<br>
a subtle glow is growing<br>
inside the heart's heart.<br>
</font>
</td></tr></tbody></table>

<a name="SeekinguntilFound"><h4>Seeking until Found</h4></a> 
<font size="1"><b>Reading by Alan Harris (<a href="../audio/seeking-until-found.wma">wma</a> | <a href="../audio/seeking-until-found.mp3">mp3</a>)</b></font><br><br>
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
There is a footless path,<br>
a carless road,<br>
a planeless flight<br>
to a placeless mountain<br>
within.<br>
<br>
When focused on our outer joys<br>
we seek after things that weigh or thrill,<br>
we dignify the use of force,<br>
we laud coarse lucre with our hopes.<br>
Seeking without, we remain without.<br>
<br>
If we but listen quietly<br>
for the call to an inner mountain state,<br>
we find that our souls are known and loved<br>
by a subtle shepherd grooming us<br>
to serve and build, to sow and reap.<br>
<br>
Knowing our knownness,<br>
we may find our foundness.<br>
</font>

<a name="Plowhorse"><h4>Plowhorse</h4></a>

<a name="Door2"><h4>Door</h4></a>
<font size="1"><b>Reading by Alan Harris (<a href="../audio/door.wma">wma</a> | <a href="../audio/door.mp3">mp3</a>)</b></font><br><br>
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
At the far end<br>
of this sun-dappled,<br>
wisteria-draped courtyard<br>
I see a Romanesque<br>
wooden door, slightly open,<br>
revealing light from behind.<br>
<br>
This courtyard is a lovely place<br>
but the door invites me further.<br>
Do I dare approach this portal<br>
and open it?  Walk through?<br>
Will my future change?<br>
Why am I so beckoned?<br>
<br>
I push open the door and enter.<br>
<br>
Two attendants lead me<br>
directly to an oaken podium<br>
set before a large audience<br>
of robed men and women.<br>
I am asked to give a speech.<br>
<br>
Quietly I say to everyone:<br>
"A speech I cannot give,<br>
kind friends.  There was<br>
an outer door I saw ajar,<br>
and I came boldly through,<br>
but I am no one<br>
you would listen to."<br>
<br>
The same attendants<br>
help me don a robe,<br>
then lead me to a chair<br>
among the listeners.<br>
<br>
We all sit and wait.<br>
</font>
</td></tr></tbody></table>

<a name="Outwhere"><h4>Outwhere</h4></a> 
<font size="1"><b>Reading by Alan Harris (<a href="../audio/outwhere.wma">wma</a> | <a href="../audio/outwhere.mp3">mp3</a>)</b></font><br><br>

<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
A rocket breaking free<br>
from Earth's gravity is,<br>
by dint of direction,<br>
traveling a trajectory<br> 
into outwhere.<br>
			<br>
No limit is seen<br>
to what is outer,<br>
but what is inner<br>
offers with its<br>

and a promise.<br>
<br>
Let rocket people<br>
point their probing<br>
within if they would<br>
make discoveries.<br>
<br>
Far-going rockets<br>
may be today's<br>
Tower of Babel<br>
reaching out and up<br>
to an imagined<br>
material heaven while,<br>
nearer than our nuclei,<br>
heaven is hugging us.<br>

<a name="PassingandPausing"><h4>Passing and Pausing</h4></a> 
<font size="1"><b>Reading by Alan Harris (<a href="../audio/passing-and-pausing.wma">wma</a> | <a href="../audio/passing-and-pausing.mp3">mp3</a>)</b></font><br><br>
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
Do you think this lived-in "Now"<br>
could be any more about self?<br>
Toys and joys, thrills and kills<br>
all decorate our deadly days.<br>
<br>
"Now's" cousin "Then" was mayhem<br>
aptly captured between bookends,<br>
whereas "Will be" rides veiled on high<br>
like cirrus clouds above the moon.<br>
<br>
With the past a mess for certain<br>
and the present a certain mess,<br>
our trust must be in the future<br>
beginning no later than here.<br>
<br>
Passing, pausing through life and life,<br>
caught up in matter's unloveliness,<br>
we still need to stay and work<br>
and be, yes be--linked in good heart<br>
as we walk on the road into Light.<br>
</font>
</td></tr></tbody></table>

<a name="AVision"><h4>A Vision</h4></a> 
<font size="1"><b>Reading by Alan Harris (<a href="../audio/a-vision.wma">wma</a> | <a href="../audio/a-vision.mp3">mp3</a>)</b></font><br><br>
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
Our new world is coming,<br>
devoid of rage,<br>
with creatures not eaten<br>
and guns melted down.<br>
<br>
Its two-party system<br>
is cordial and fair--<br>
the Forwardists move<br>
as the Holdists delay.<br>
<br>
The trade is quite honest<br>
and arguing's rare<br>
as the selfish now give,<br>
the ambitious now serve.<br>
<br>
How can this world<br>
ever work? you may ask.<br>
Aren't giving and serving<br>
quite dull? you inquire.<br>
<br>
We will see as we go,<br>
but the strife in the old,<br>
based on you, me, and them,<br>
was a nightmare of self.<br>
<br>
What mattered the most<br>
was mostly matter,<br>
that dubious deity<br>
for eyes that see down.<br>
<br>
Our new world is coming<br>
between all the bullets<br>
and bombs--yes, coming<br>
as surely as daylight.<br>
</font>
</td></tr></tbody></table>

<a name="FatherHowCanIHearYou"><h4>Father, How Can I Hear You?</h4></a> 
<h6>A Song of Renewal</h6>
<h6><a href="../audio/father.htm">Recorded Musical Duet</a></h6>
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
Father, Father, how can I hear You?<br>
Why are the clouds so gray?<br>
Why is the wind so cold?<br>
Oh, why are the trees so bare?<br>
Father, Father, how can I hear You?<br>
<br>
Father, Father, I pray unto You.<br>
I pray for Your light, but the clouds remain;<br>
I pray for Your warmth, but the cold wind blows on;<br>
I pray for new growth, but the trees are still bare;<br>
Father, Father, I pray unto You.<br>
<br>
Father, Father, now I hear Your voice.<br>
Your sun melts away my clouds, and I see Your light;<br>
Your warm breath replaces the freezing wind;<br>
The trees are beginning to bud and flower;<br>
The landscape grows green with Your love.<br>
Father, Father, now I can hear You.<br>
</font>
</td></tr></tbody></table>

<a name="EnglishTeacherUnbound"><h4>English Teacher Unbound</h4></a>
<font size="1"><b>Reading by Alan Harris (<a href="../audio/english-teacher-unbound.wma">wma</a> | <a href="../audio/english-teacher-unbound.mp3">mp3</a>)</b></font><br><br>
<table><tbody><tr><td>
<font face="VERDANA,ARIAL,HELVETICA" size="-1">
Dickinson. Frost. Eliot.<br>
Wonderful vetted poets--<br>
but sameness of names<br>
in every school.<br>
My students are alive--<br>
they need MEANING,<br>
not biography-worship.<br>
<br>
Bless Keats and<br>
jolly Shakespeare<br>
for all they wrote--<br>
but now let's dare<br>
to anonymize these<br>
bards around whom<br>
schools have<br>
mummified their<br>
curricula by means<br>
of committee after<br>
workgroup<br> 
kowtowing to<br>
conformist after<br>
department head<br>
after principal as<br>
the decades ditto on.<br>
<br>
I'd rather pluck<br>
new writings out of<br>
most abundant<br>
everywhere,<br>
throw them all<br>
nameless into<br>
a vibrant pile,<br>
then pull them up<br>
one by three--<br>
READ them--<br>
BE them--<br>
poems and stories<br>
written by unknowns<br>
who may inspire<br>
and kindle fire.<br>
<br>
I fully CARE,<br>
but I'm captive<br>
in this well-lit,<br>
firmly-administered,<br>
climate-controlled<br>
classtomb.<br>
<br>
SOULS come here,<br>
parched souls.<br>
We're to feed them<br>
stacks of<br>
cardboard facts<br>
and poetic forms<br>
to memorize--<br>
vital to know,<br>
we con, because<br>
they'll be on<br>
the final exam.<br>
<br>
Teachers, let us<br>
wake very much up!<br>
Dare we transcend<br>
the tried and dead?<br>
<br>
Let's each write a sonnet<br>
on why we don't read<br>
sonnets--or an elegy<br>
for the deceased<br>
meanings of passion.<br>
<br>
What would Shakespeare<br>
write about our schools?<br>
"Much Ado about Atrophy"?<br>
And Robert Frost?<br>
"The Railroad Not Taken"?<br>
<br>
I am nobody<br>
to be writing like this,<br>
nor am I in your syllabus,<br>
but I can still breathe.<br>
</font>
</td></tr></tbody></table>
