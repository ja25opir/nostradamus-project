      <h2><a name="top"></a>New Campaign Touts Benefits of Standards</h2>
      <p>Looking to prove the importance of
        standards to your company’s executives?
      Here’s some help.</p>
      <p>        <a href="www.standardsboostbusiness.org" target="_blank"><img src="../../../art/anews/2010.sep/boost.jpg" alt="Standards Boost Business Web Site" width="200" height="127" hspace="15" border="0" align="right"></a>AAMI is sponsoring the Standards
        Boost Business campaign that was
        launched by the American National
        Standards Institute (ANSI) to show how
        standards can help cut costs, improve
        efficiency, and maintain market access.
        The campaign promotes the website
        <a href="http://www.standardsboostbusiness.org" target="_blank">www.standardsboostbusiness.org</a>.</p>
      <p>“AAMI members sometimes tell us
        that senior executives in their organization
        have difficulty seeing the direct
        benefit and importance of participation
        in standards development work,” says
        AAMI President Mary Logan.</p>
      <p>        The Standards Boost Business campaign
        gives AAMI members a toolkit to
        demonstrate why standards and standards
        participation are essential business
        tools, Logan adds. “The campaign
        should help increase the visibility of
        standards development work, which is
        good for all of us.”</p>
      <p>        The toolkit, available for free on the 
      website, includes:</p>
      <ul>
        <li> A PowerPoint presentation to show 
          executives</li>
        <li> A list of standards that can improve 
          productivity and reduce costs</li>
        <li> A question-and-answer document 
        for executives</li>
      </ul>
      <p>        The toolkit offers a helpful reminder 
        about the long-term benefits of standards, 
        especially in the current economic 
        climate. “Standards work doesn’t 
        bear fruit for years to come,” says 
        Charles Sidebottom, director of corporate 
        standards at Medtronic in 
        Minneapolis, MN, and a member of<br>
        AAMI’s Board of Directors. “We need 
        to constantly remind people internally, 
        and keep refreshing the idea that standards 
        work is a strategic initiative, and 
      we are investing in the future.”</p>
      <p>        Sidebottom adds this is particularly 
        true in a regulated industry — such as 
        the medical device industry — that uses 
        voluntary consensus standards as part 
      of the regulatory approval process.</p>
      <p>        ANSI officials were appreciative of 
        the sponsorship from AAMI. “Joining
        as a sponsor signals AAMI understands
        and values the critical role that
        standards and conformity assessment
        play — not just in the medical instrumentation
        field, but throughout the
        global marketplace,” says S. Joe Bhatia,
        ANSI president and chief executive
        officer (CEO).</p>
      <p class="text"><em>AAMI News</em>: September 2010, Vol. 45, No. 9</p>
